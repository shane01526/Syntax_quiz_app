本章是全書最後一章，把第 9 章開頭的 wh- 移位再往前推：雖然 wh- 移位看似可以把片語移到任意遠的距離，但 Adger 要論證——這種「長距離移位」其實永遠是由一連串短的、局域的小步組成，而非一次長跳躍。為此他引入階段（phase）的概念：句法推導是一塊一塊（chunk by chunk）進行的，每個階段一旦完成，其內部（除了「邊緣」＝指示語）就對後續運算不可見。這條原則叫 階段不可穿透條件（PIC）。本章最後用它＋幾條補充原則，統一解釋傳統上一大堆島嶼限制（island constraints）：wh- 島嶼、複合名詞片語（DP）島嶼、主語島嶼、附加語島嶼。

本章的核心問題

第 9 章末留下一個問題：當 wh- 片語跨越一個中間子句邊界時，它是一步跳到底（long swoop），還是兩步（或多步）逐層往上爬（successive cyclic）？兩種表徵如下：

(1) [CP XP[wh] C[uwh*] … V [CP (XP[wh]) C … (XP[wh]) ]]　← 局域、逐層的 wh- 移位
(2) [CP XP[wh] C[uwh*] … V [CP C … (XP[wh]) ]]　← 一步到底的長距離 wh- 移位 (1) 走兩步：wh-XP 先從句中某位置（甚至可以是已派生的 Spec-TP）移到內嵌 CP 的指示語，再從那裡移到主句 CP 的指示語。(2) 則「跳過」中間的 Spec-CP，一口氣移上去。Adger 在上一章已主張 (1) 才對，本章要再補強這個立論，並引入標準術語：這種逐層往上「循環」CP 的推導，叫 連續循環移位（successive cyclic movement）。

10.2 局域移位的經驗證據

書頁 377

本節提出三組互相獨立的論證，都指向「wh- 移位是連續循環的」：(i) 一種英語方言裡的浮游量詞；(ii) 蘇格蘭蓋爾語的補語連詞交替；(iii) 西班牙語的主語倒裝。三者合起來說明：wh- 移位確實是「一個 Spec-CP 接一個 Spec-CP」地往上走的。

10.2.1 浮游量詞（West Ulster English）

書頁 377–378

第一個證據來自北愛爾蘭西厄斯特（West Ulster）方言。此方言可把量詞 all 黏附在 wh- 詞上：

(3) What all did you get for Christmas?
(4) Who all did you meet when you were in Derry?
(5) Where all did they go for their holidays?

回想第 6 章：主語的量詞可以被遺留（strand）在 vP 內基底位置，這曾被當成「主語是 VP 內部合併」的證據。同理，黏在 wh- 詞上的 all 也可以被遺留：

(6) What did you get all for Xmas?
(7) Who did you meet all when you were in Derry?
(8) Where did they go all for their holidays?

內嵌問句也一樣：

(9) I don't remember [what all I said].
(10) I don't remember [what I said all]. 假設 [what all] 整個是在基底位置（θ位置）合併的成分；接著可以只把 wh- 片語 what 拉走，把量詞 all 留在原地。(6) 的結構大致是：[vP get [DP all (what)]]——what 上移、all 留下。
(11) [vP get [DP all (what) ]]　← (6) 的 vP 內結構：what 已上移、all 遺留在基底
書頁 378

關鍵來了。如果 wh- 片語在長距離移位時先停靠在中間的 Spec-CP，那我們應該也能把量詞遺留在那個中間位置。事實正是如此——下面三組對比，分別把 all 留在基底、整移、或留在中間 Spec-CP：

(12) What all did he say [(that) he wanted]?　（整移）
(13) What did he say [(that) he wanted all]?　（遺留在基底）
(14) What did he say [all (that) he wanted]?　（遺留在中間 Spec-CP！）
(15) Where do you think [all they'll want to visit]?
(16) What do they claim [all that we did]? (14)–(16) 的 all 既不在主句、也不在最深的基底，而是落在內嵌子句的最前緣（中間 Spec-CP）。這只有在「wh- 片語確實先停靠過那個位置」時才解釋得通。

而且系統正確預測：被遺留的量詞應位於補語連詞 that 之前，而非其後（因為 Spec-CP 在 C 之前）：

(17) * What did he say [that all he wanted]? (17) 把 all 放在 that 之後就壞了——因為遺留位置是 Spec-CP（在 that=C 之前），不是 C 與 TP 之間。基本結構如 (18)。
書頁 379
(18) [CP what … [CP (what) all C [TP … (what all) ]]] 這條結構同時放了兩個 (what) 痕跡：一個在中間 Spec-CP（遺留 all 的位置），一個在基底。若移位一步到底、跳過中間位置，這些句子根本生不出來——因為「all 出現在中間 Spec-CP」這個結構從未被建出來過。所以我們要嘛放棄量詞遺留分析，要嘛放棄「一步跳過中間 Spec-CP」的主張。

這個分析還預測：量詞也應能被遺留在非限定 C 的指示語。正確：

(19) What were you trying all to say?
(20) What did you mean all for me to do? 遺留的 all 又出現在非限定補語連詞 for 之前，正如「wh- 移位總會停靠 Spec-CP」所預期。但要注意：西厄斯特英語的證據只證明「必須允許連續循環移位」，它並沒有排除「一步到底」也存在——它只說明「一步到底」不可能是唯一的方式。下一個論證則更強。

10.2.2 補語連詞一致再探（Scottish Gaelic）

書頁 379–380

第 9 章提過：蘇格蘭蓋爾語裡內嵌補語連詞會「換形」，可追溯到「wh- 移位必須連續循環」。本節把這個論證加強。首先，沒有 wh- 移位的句子，內嵌補語連詞是 gu(n)：

(21) Bha mi ag radh gun do bhuail e. was I asp saying that prt struck she him ／「我那時在說她打了他。」
(22) Tha mi a' smaoineachadh gu bheil Iain air a mhisg. am I asp thinking that-is Iain on his drink ／「我認為 Iain 喝醉了。」 假設 gu(n) 的特徵就是 [C, finite]（限定 C，不帶 wh）。
書頁 380

與英語不同，蓋爾語的主句 wh- 問句有一個顯性補語連詞 a（wh- 詞以母音結尾時不發音，否則發音）：

(23) Co an duine a tha thu a' posadh? who the man C are you asp marrying ／「你要娶哪個男人？」
(24) Co a' chaileag a tha thu a' pogadh? who the girl C are you asp kissing ／「你在親哪個女孩？」 分析：a 至少帶 [C, uwh*]，與英語對應的問句 C 一樣，只是缺 [Q]（因為同一個 a 也用於分裂句與關係子句，這些都不是問句）。據此可立下概括 (26)。
(25) CP / \ Co an duine C' / \ a[uwh*] TP / \ tha thu … v / VP … a' pogadh (Co an duine)
(26) 概括：若 Spec-CP 被 wh- 片語填充，則該 CP 由補語連詞 a 領導；否則由 gu 領導。
書頁 381

現在看從內嵌子句抽取的情形。當成分從內嵌子句 wh- 移到主句 Spec-CP 時，原本該領導內嵌子句的 gu 消失，改成 a（內嵌的 a 以粗體標出）：

(27) Co an duine a bha thu ag radh a bhuail i? who the man C were you asp saying that struck she ／「你那時在說她打了哪個男人？」
(28) Co am bodach a tha thu a' smaoineachadh a tha air a mhisg? who the old-man C are you asp thinking that-is on his drink ／「你認為哪個老頭喝醉了？」 概括 (26) 立刻告訴我們：移位必定停靠過內嵌 Spec-CP（否則內嵌 C 不會變成 a）。到此還與西厄斯特英語一樣。但蓋爾語的論證更強——見下。

關鍵：若 wh- 移位已發生但內嵌仍用 gu(n)，句子不合語法：

(29) * Co an duine a bha thu ag radh gun do bhuail i?
(30) * Co am bodach a tha thu a' smaoineachadh gu bheil air a mhisg? 這顯示：停靠中間 Spec-CP 不只是「可選」、而是「強制」。若不假設此停靠，就無法解釋 (29)(30) 為何不合語法。與量詞論證的差別：西厄斯特資料只「不排除」一步到底；但蓋爾語的補語連詞一致明確排除一步到底——因為若移位一步到底，就無法阻止「內嵌用 gu」的 (29)(30)。

10.2.3 連續倒裝現象（Spanish）

書頁 382

第三個論證與蓋爾語同型，來自西班牙語的主語倒裝。西班牙語（與義大利語）可選地允許主語出現在動詞片語之後：

(31) Maria contestó la pregunta.Maria answered the question ／「Maria 回答了問題。」
(32) Contestó la pregunta Maria.answered the question Maria ／（同義，主語後置）

但在 wh- 問句中，這個倒裝不是可選的，而是強制的：

(33) ✓ Qué querían esos dos?what wanted those two ／「那兩個人想要什麼？」
(34) * Qué esos dos querían?what those two wanted 概括 (35)：當 wh- 詞在 Spec-CP，倒裝強制；否則可選。我們可把它當「探針」——若倒裝強制 ⇔ Spec-CP 被填，那麼長距離問句中，每一個被 wh- 經過的子句都應出現倒裝。
(35) 概括：當 wh- 詞在 Spec-CP 時，倒裝強制；否則可選。
書頁 383

先看一個多層內嵌的陳述句（主語 Pedro、la revista 都在動詞之前）：

(36) Juan pensaba que Pedro le había dicho que la revista había publicado ya el artículo. 「Juan 以為 Pedro 已告訴他那本期刊已經登了那篇文章。」

若把最深的賓語 el artículo wh- 抽取出來，會發現每一個中間子句都發生主語倒裝：

(37) ✓ Qué pensaba Juan que le había dicho Pedro que había publicado la revista? 「Juan 以為 Pedro 告訴他那本期刊登了什麼？」 主句主語 Juan 跟在動詞 pensaba 後、中間主語 Pedro 跟在 dicho 後、最深主語 la revista 也跟在其動詞後——三層全倒裝。
(38) * Qué pensaba Juan que Pedro le había dicho que la revista había publicado? (38) 中間子句沒有倒裝（Pedro、la revista 仍在動詞前）→ 不合語法。根據概括 (35)，(37) 的推導必須讓 wh- 片語停靠在每一個中間 Spec-CP，否則倒裝不會強制。(38) 缺倒裝即不合語法，這正說明「一步到底」的推導不可用。
書頁 384
10.2 小結：三組獨立證據（浮游量詞、補語連詞一致、主語倒裝）一致指向同一結論：長距離 wh- 移位不是一步到底，而是逐層經過每一個 Spec-CP；而且這些 Spec-CP 不可跳過。不需要知道倒裝的確切機制，只要看到「強制倒裝 ⇔ Spec-CP 被填」即可外推。

10.3 確保連續移位的機制

書頁 384

上一節的論證都指向同一方向，但怎麼用理論把它做出來、同時解決「經濟性（Economy）」問題？本節組織理論來捕捉這個想法。

10.3.1 特徵匹配的局域性與 PIC

書頁 384–385

回想：wh- 移位由 C 上一個強的、不可解讀的 wh- 特徵觸發。單句裡，這個 wh- 特徵可匹配同句中某成分的 wh- 特徵：

(39) C[uwh*] … XP[wh]

但上節論證顯示：匹配運算無法伸進下層子句，至多只能「看到」那個子句的指示語。換句話說，任一 C 的補語（complement）對特徵匹配是隱形的——(40) 裡較高的 F 永遠檢核不到；除非把 XP[F] 移到 Spec-CP，匹配才成立 (41)：

(40) ✗ C[uF] … [CP C [TP … XP[F] … ]]　← F 藏在補語深處，看不到
(41) ✓ C[uF] … [CP XP[F] C [TP … (XP[F]) … ]]　← F 升到 Spec-CP，可被匹配
(42) 假設：c-統制下的特徵檢核，至多伸到任一內嵌 CP 的指示語。 這條 (42) 只排除了「跳過中間 Spec-CP 的長移位」，但它本身並不能「逼」任何東西先移進那個中間 Spec-CP。要逼出這個移位，需要第二個假設。
書頁 385

第二步：那些「顯性標記中間位置」的語言（如蓋爾語），其實是把句法特徵「寫在形態的袖子上」。正如英語 DP 帶抽象格（無語音實現的格特徵），我們假設英語的內嵌 C 可選地帶一個抽象 [uwh*]（只是英語不在形態上標記它）：

(43) 內嵌 C 可選地帶 [uwh*]。（視為一條 UG 原則） (43) 蘊涵：每個語言都有兩種（相關的）C：一種帶 [uwh*]、一種不帶。有些語言把這兩個 C 拼寫得不同（蓋爾語：a vs gu），有些不（英語），但這兩個功能中心語的選擇永遠存在。
書頁 385–386

把 (42) + (43) 合起來，就能強制連續移位。設想已建好一個含 wh-XP 的 CP (44)；把它嵌在動詞下、最終又被一個帶 [uwh*] 的主句 C 包住。若內嵌用不帶 [uwh*] 的 C：因為檢核看不過下一層 C 的指示語，主句 wh- 特徵永遠匹配不到 → 推導崩潰（crash）。若內嵌選用帶 [uwh*] 的 C：此強特徵會與 XP 的 wh- 特徵匹配，強制 wh-XP 移進下層 Spec-CP (45)：

(44) C … XP[wh]
(45) XP[wh] C[uwh*] … (XP[wh])　← 強 [uwh*] 把 XP 拉進下層 Spec-CP 一旦 wh-XP 站到下層 Spec-CP，它就可被上層 C 匹配——於是檢核較高那個強 wh- 特徵。這就「逼出」了連續循環移位：每一層都先用帶 [uwh*] 的 C 把 wh- 拉到自己的 Spec，再供更上層使用。
書頁 386

這個「特徵匹配是局域的」想法通常從 CP 推廣開來：10.4 會看到 DP 同樣如此，PP 在某些語言也是。我們把這些有此行為的範疇叫 階段（phase），並把概括改寫為：

(46) 特徵匹配至多伸到一個內嵌階段（phase）的指示語。
階段不可穿透條件（Phase Impenetrability Constraint, PIC）：(46) 即 PIC。Chomsky 主張：階段這個概念可從推導的一般結構導出——本質上是逼推導「記性很短」：一旦完成一個階段，就忘掉其內部（補語）的東西，只保留邊緣（指示語）可見。本書不深入此推導，而是去看「階段」如何解釋各種移位限制。

10.3.2 解一個舊難題：不當移位

書頁 386–387

第 8 章 8.2.6 看提升動詞時留下一個難題：把一個限定 CP 嵌在提升動詞 seem 下 (47)。若合併 T[uD*, nom] 後，讓 T 的 [nom] 與 [EPP] 跟內嵌主語 Medea 匹配，似乎就能把 Medea 提升到主句主語位置，得出 (48)：

(47) vP / \ v VP / \ seem CP / \ that TP Medea has poisoned Jason
(48) * Medea seemed that has poisoned Jason.
(49) [TP T[uD*] seemed [CP (that) Medea[D, nom] has poisoned Jason ]] 有了 PIC，(48) 立刻被排除：Medea 不是 wh- 片語，不會移進下層 Spec-CP，所以永遠不在邊緣、對主句 T 的 EPP 不可見。因此 (48) 無法從 (49) 派生出來——這正是第 8 章難題的解答。
書頁 387

但這帶出同一問題的新版本：wh- 片語確實會移進中間 Spec-CP，那為何 (50) 仍不合語法？

(50) * Who seemed (that) had poisoned Jason?

下面的推導 (51) 表面上應該行得通：

  1. 建 TP：[TP who poisoned Jason]。
  2. 合併 C[uwh*]，把 who 升到其指示語：[CP who[D, wh, nom] C[uwh*] … ]。
  3. 合併 seem、v、T[uD*, nom]。
  4. 讓 T 的 EPP/格與 who 的 D 匹配，把 who 升到主句 Spec-TP：[TP who[D, wh, nom] T seemed [CP (who) (that) (who) had poisoned Jason ]]。
  5. 合併主句 C，把 who 升到其指示語。
(51) 上述五步推導（見上方編號清單）。 這個推導唯一「異常」的一步，是 who 從 Spec-CP 移到 Spec-TP。生成語法傳統把「從 Spec-CP 移到其他種類位置」的移位稱為 不當移位（improper movement）。
書頁 388
(52) 不當移位限制（Improper Movement Restriction）：在 Spec-CP 裡，只有 wh- 特徵可見。 這條限制排除了 (51) 中那一步：T 的 EPP 無法跟 Spec-CP 裡 who 的 D 特徵匹配（因為在 Spec-CP 只有 wh- 可見）。當然，若 who 留在 Spec-TP，則離主句 C 太遠、也匹配不到。Adger 坦言：這條限制目前仍是規約性的（stipulation），其更深的解釋是當前理論辯論的課題，但對本書夠用。

10.4 島嶼現象

書頁 388

本節研究其他與 wh- 移位有關的局域性效應——也就是傳統所謂的島嶼（islands）：某些成分像「島」一樣，wh- 移位無法「游出去」。

10.4.1 Wh- 島嶼

書頁 388–389

第 9 章看過多個 wh- 片語的情形：最高的 wh- 片語升到主句 Spec-CP，較低者不動（匹配運算鎖定最近的 wh- 片語，即優越性 superiority），故 (54) 不合法：

(53) Who C (who) T … (who) saw what?
(54) * What did who (did) … (who) see (what)?

同一效應出現在內嵌子句：

(55) ✓ I asked who saw what.
(56) * I asked what who saw?
書頁 389

本章系統預測：若某個 wh- 片語已佔了內嵌 Spec-CP，那更深處的 wh- 片語就無法再往上移。相關配置如 (57)：

(57) … V [CP XP1[wh] … XP2[wh] … ] 只有 XP1 能移進主句 Spec-CP，原因有二：① 主句 C 吸引最近的 wh-（優越性）；② 更深的 XP2 不在 CP 階段的指示語上（被 PIC 擋住）。
(58) * What did you ask who saw (what)?

雙賓動詞同理：

(59) ✓ I asked who Medea gave what?
(60) * What did you ask who Medea gave (what)?
(61) 概括：無法把一個 wh- 片語移出「指示語已被另一 wh- 片語佔據」的 CP。 這就是文獻中古老的 Wh- 島嶼限制（Wh-Island Constraint）：被「指示語已有 wh-」的 CP 包住的 wh-，像被困在島上，「下不了島」。注意 island ≠ phase：phase 是理論術語（只有指示語可被匹配的範疇）；island 只是描述術語（描述「無法 wh- 移出」的成分），本身不告訴你「為什麼」。
書頁 389–390

用 D- 連結（D-linked）的 wh- 表達式，島嶼效應看得更清楚（非 D- 連結時，常分不清是優越性還是 wh- 島嶼）。D- 連結可避開優越性，使低 wh- 越過高 wh-：

(62) ✓ I asked which king invaded which city.
(63) ✓ I asked which city which king invaded.
(64) [CP which city C[uwh*] [TP which king invaded (which city) ]] (63)(64) 顯示 object wh-（which city）可避開優越性、升到內嵌 Spec-CP。既然它已在階段的指示語，理論預測它應能再往上移——(65) 證實此預測（多數人覺得略降，但與 (66) 對比戲劇化）：
(65) ? Which city did you ask which king invaded?
(66) * Which king did you ask which city invaded? (66) 只剩「城市入侵國王」這個與題意無關（且語用怪異）的解讀；在與 (63) 同義的解讀下完全不合法。理論正確預測：內嵌主語（which king）不在 CP 階段的指示語上（它在 Spec-TP，被 which city 佔了 Spec-CP），故移不出去。注意這不能歸因於優越性，因為 wh- 主語 DP 是 D- 連結的。
書頁 390

離開 wh- 島嶼前，理論還預測下列句子良構（把內嵌主語先升到內嵌 Spec-CP，再升到主句 Spec-CP，不違反 PIC）：

(67) ✓ Who did you ask saw what?
(68) ✓ Which king did you wonder invaded which city? 這兩句略難剖析，但與上面的 wh- 島嶼違規對比鮮明。結論：傳統的 wh- 島嶼限制可由 PIC 捕捉。

10.4.2 DP 島嶼（複合名詞片語島嶼）

書頁 391

另一類島嶼涉及 DP。三種情形——名詞帶 CP、PP、of-DP 論元：

(69) ✓ I believed [DP the claim [CP that Philip would invade the city of Athens ]].
(70) * Which city do you believe [DP the claim [CP that Philip would invade ]]?
(71) ✓ Plato listened to [DP Demosthenes' oration [PP about Philip ]].
(72) * Who did Plato listen to [DP Demosthenes' oration [PP about ]]?
(73) ✓ Phaedo was interested in [DP Plato's description [of geometry] ].
(74) * What was Phaedo interested in [DP Plato's description [of] ]? 三例都是某 XP（CP / PP / of-DP）當名詞的論元，從中 wh- 移出都遠比理論預期糟。傳統稱 複合名詞片語島嶼（Complex Noun Phrase Islands）（此名稱略過時，因今天視之為完整 DP 而非 NP）。
書頁 391–392

捕捉方式：假設 DP 與 CP 一樣是階段。如此一來，DP 內任何 wh- 表達式都對「之後合併的 [uwh*] C」不可見（PIC：除了指示語，階段內一切不可匹配）。上述案例中沒有任何 wh- 在 Spec-DP，故理論正確預測 wh- 移出不可能。以 (75) 的 CP 補語案例為例：

(75) * Which city do you believe [DP the claim [CP that Philip would invade ]]?
(76) [CP which city that[uwh*] [TP Philip would invade (which city) ]]
(77) [CP [do C[Q,uwh*]] you believe [DP the claim [CP which city that[uwh*] [TP Philip would invade (which city) ]]]] 即使 which city 已升到內嵌 Spec-CP (76)，當這個 CP 被裝進 DP (the claim …)、DP 又是階段時，which city 就被埋在 DP 的補語裡（不在 Spec-DP），主句 C 的 wh- 特徵匹配不到 (77) → 不合法。
書頁 392–393

能不能在 D 上放 [uwh*]、把 wh- 拉到 Spec-DP？以名詞的 CP 補語為例 (78)，須先把 which city 移到補語 CP 的指示語，再移到 Spec-DP (79)。但這要求 the 能承載 [uwh*]——而這似乎錯誤：定冠詞 DP 內找不到 DP 內部 wh- 移位：

(78) * Which city do you believe the claim that Philip would invade?
(79) [DP [which city] the claim that Philip would invade ]　← 假想的中間結構
(80) * Which god the statue
(81) * How fierce the battle (80)(81) 顯示 the 不能承載 [uwh*]。所有定冠詞（含檢核屬格的零冠詞）都一樣，都產生島嶼效應：
(82) * Which poem did you hear Homer's recital of last night?
(83) * Which poem did you hear those recitals of last night?
書頁 393

但不定冠詞不同——從不定 DP 抽取良好：

(84) ✓ Which poem did you go to hear a recital of last night?
(85) ✓ Who did you get an accurate description of?
(86) ✓ Who did you hear an oration about? 概括：定指 DP 是島嶼，非定指 DP 不是。兩種可能解釋：(i) 階段性取決於 D 的定指性；(ii) 不定 D 允許 wh- 指示語（如 C 一樣可選帶 [uwh*]）。

支持第二種的證據：確實找得到 DP 內部 wh- 移位，且似乎只限不定 DP：

(87) a fierce battle ～ ✓ How fierce a battle
(88) the fierce battle ～ * How fierce the battle
(89) an oration about Philip ～ * Who an oration about (89) 顯示這種 DP 內部移位不適用於名詞的論元，所以兩種解釋都還有問題。Adger 在此暫不定論，姑且假設只有定指 DP 是階段。

10.4.3 主語島嶼

書頁 393–394

第三類是主語島嶼。用形容詞 obvious（帶單一 CP 論元）：(90) CP 作主語、(91) 用虛主語 it：

(90) That Plato loved Aster was obvious.
(91) It was obvious that Plato loved Aster.

可從 (91) wh- 移出 love 的賓語，但不能從 (90)：

(92) ✓ Who was it obvious that Plato loved?
(93) * Who was that Plato loved obvious?

CP 當動詞主語時同理：

(94) That Plato loved Aster seemed to be known by everyone.
(95) * Who did that Plato loved seem to be known by everyone?
(96) That Plato loved Aster proved to be his undoing.
(97) * Who did that Plato loved prove to be his undoing?
書頁 394

不只賓語——從 CP 主語裡什麼都抽不出來（主語、介系詞賓語、方式副詞皆然）：

(98) a. That Plato loved Aster was obvious. ／ b. * Who was that loved Aster obvious?
(99) a. That Plato lived in the city of Athens was well known. ／ b. * Which city was that Plato lived in well known?
(100) a. That Plato loved Aster deeply was obvious. ／ b. * How deeply was that Plato loved Aster obvious?
(101) 句子主語島嶼限制（Sentential Subject Island Constraint）：什麼都不能移出一個子句主語。
書頁 394–395

從 seem 的非句首 CP 論元抽取（如 (91)）則正確被預測為良好：先建內嵌 CP、選 C[uwh*] 把 who 移到其指示語、再與 obvious 合併 (102)；繼續合併 be 與虛主語後，who 可移到主句 Spec-CP，得良構的 (103)：

(102) AP / \ obvious CP / \ who C' / \ that TP Plato loved (who)
(103) ✓ Who was it obvious that Plato loved?

那麼句子主語為何是島？看兩種句子主語分析：(a) 在 Spec-TP；(b) 基底附加到 TP、Spec-TP 由空代詞填。

書頁 395

(a) 在 Spec-TP 的分析：把 obvious 的 CP 論元移進 Spec-TP (104)，合併 C[Q,uwh*] 並升助動詞，再把（位於主語 CP 指示語、即「相關階段指示語」的）who 升到主句 Spec-CP (105)。推導收斂、所有特徵都檢核了，句子卻完全不合法——此分析下主語島嶼無解：

(104) [TP [who that Plato loved] was obvious ]
(105) CP / \ who C' / \ C[Q,uwh*] TP / \ [CP (who) that T' Plato loved] / \ was PredP (CP) obvious → 推導收斂，卻完全不合語法：(a) 分析失敗
書頁 395–396

(b) 替代分析：子句主語在一個特殊 CP 的指示語，Spec-TP 由空代詞 PRO 填 (106)。此結構無法再做成 wh- 問句（因為該 CP 的指示語已被子句主語佔據），也無法再被嵌套（此特殊 C 只在主句合法）。於是句子主語島嶼從獨立動機的子句主語分析自然導出——是好事：

(106) CP / \ CP C' / \ / \ (who) C' C[Q,uwh*] TP / \ PRO[null] was obvious that TP Plato loved (who)
書頁 396

但句子主語其實只是主語更一般性質的一部分——一般而言，什麼都抽不出主語：

(107) a. A programme about Euripides is on Radio 4 tonight. ／ b. * Who is a programme about on Radio 4 tonight?
(108) a. There is a programme about Euripides on Radio 4 tonight. ／ b. ✓ Who is there a programme about on Radio 4 tonight? 記得從不定 DP 可以抽取（10.4.2）。(108) 用虛主語 there 把 DP 留在低位，抽取就良好；(107) 把同一 DP 放在 Spec-TP 主語位，抽取就降格。對比顯示：從 Spec-TP 裡的成分抽取會降格。
書頁 396–397

一種解法訴諸：主語不在「被中心語選擇／指派 θ- 角色」的位置。Spec-TP 是非 θ 位置（不像 Spec-vP 或補語位）。據此立下約束 (109)：

(109) 一個階段的指示語，只有當該階段位於「被 θ- 指派中心語選擇」的位置時，才對特徵匹配可見。
書頁 397

運作示範。先看合法的 (110)：建內嵌 CP、who 移到其指示語、CP 與 obvious 合併（obvious 指派 θ- 角色給它）。因為內嵌 CP 在被 θ- 指派中心語選擇的位置，其指示語可見，who 可順利 wh- 移出 (111 樹)：

(110) ✓ Who is it obvious that Plato loves?
(111) … is PredP / \ (is) AP / \ obvious CP ← CP 被 obvious 選擇（θ位置）⇒ 指示語可見 / \ who C' / \ that TP Plato loves (who)
書頁 397–398

反之，若不合併虛主語 it，而是把內嵌 CP 移進 Spec-TP (112)：此時該 CP 不在被 θ- 指派中心語選擇的位置（T 不指派 θ- 角色），故其指示語不可見，主句 C 的 wh- 特徵檢核不到 → 推導崩潰。同樣方法也預測「從 Spec-TP 的普通 DP 主語抽取」不合法 (113)，正確導出對比 (114)：

(112) CP / \ C' TP / \ / \ C [CP who T' that / \ Plato is PredP loves(who)] AP … obvious (CP) → CP 在 Spec-TP（非 θ 位置）⇒ 指示語不可見 ⇒ crash
(113) a. A description of Aristotle is in the book. ／ b. * Who is a description of in the book?
(114) a. There is a description of Aristotle in the book. ／ b. ✓ Who is there a description of in the book?
書頁 398

注意：若 (b) 子句主語分析（特殊 CP）正確，則從該 CP 抽取會被兩條原則同時擋住（階段須在 θ 位置 + 特殊句法）。這是理論的冗餘（redundancy）——不理想（第 1 章說理論要最精簡），但本書暫且接受。至此，我們有三條關於 wh- 移位局域性的約束：

(115) 1. 特徵匹配至多伸到內嵌階段的指示語（PIC）。
2. 階段指示語只有在「該階段位於被 θ- 指派中心語選擇之處」時才對特徵匹配可見。
3. 在 Spec-CP 中只有 wh- 特徵可見（不當移位）。

10.4.4 附加語島嶼

書頁 399

最後一類是附加語島嶼。第 8.3 節見過出現在附加語位置的 CP——時間、原因、條件子句：

(116) Hephaestus had run away, before the executioner murdered Hera.
(117) Hephaestus had run away because the executioner murdered Hera.
(118) Hephaestus would run away if the executioner murdered Hera.

從這些附加語子句裡什麼都移不出來，可見它們是島：

(119) * Who had Hephaestus run away, before the executioner murdered (Hera)?
(120) * Who had Hephaestus run away, because the executioner murdered (Hera)?
(121) * Who would Hephaestus run away, if the executioner murdered (Hera)? 現行理論已經預測它們不合法：附加語從不位於「被選擇中心語 θ- 標記」的位置。所以即使引導這些子句的補語連詞可選帶 [uwh*]、把 wh- 移到其指示語，該指示語對主句 C 仍不可見（因為附加語不在 θ 位置）→ 由約束 (109) 直接排除。

10.5 本章摘要

書頁 399–401

本書最後一章探討 wh- 移位除了 Agree 上常見的介入效應（intervention effect）之外，還如何被進一步約束。

  • 先用方言英語、蘇格蘭蓋爾語、西班牙語的資料，論證 wh- 移位是連續循環的——被迫從一個 Spec-CP 走到下一個 Spec-CP。
  • 用兩個想法在理論上捕捉它：(122) c-統制下的特徵檢核至多伸到任一內嵌 CP 的指示語；(123) 內嵌 C 可選地帶強 [uwh*]。兩者皆視為 UG 的不變原則。
  • 這解決了第 8 章留下的難題：如何排除把限定 CP 主語提升進含提升動詞的高層子句——由 (122) 排除；接著的新問題（低 wh- 升進提升動詞主語）則由不當移位限制 (124)「Spec-CP 中只有 wh- 特徵可見」排除。
  • 最後把「特徵匹配看不過內嵌 CP 指示語」推廣為階段（phase），得 (125) 階段不可穿透條件（PIC）：c-統制下的特徵檢核至多伸到任一內嵌階段的指示語。這統一了 wh- 島嶼與（某些）DP 島嶼。
  • 再加 (126) 階段須在 θ 位置，其指示語才對特徵匹配可見，把主語島嶼與附加語島嶼也納入。

10.1 Introduction. This chapter pushes wh-movement further. Although wh-movement can shift a phrase an apparently unbounded distance from its launch site, it always proceeds in a number of short, local steps rather than one long leap. This builds on the Gaelic complementizer-alternation data from Ch.9. A derivation proceeds in chunks called phases, which forces wh-movement into small steps, and the phase notion will explain traditional island constraints. The Ch.9 question recurs: does wh-movement across a clausal boundary happen in one step or two+? Two competing representations:

(1) [CP XP[wh] C[uwh*]… V [CP (XP[wh]) C … (XP[wh])]] — local, stepwise
(2) [CP XP[wh] C[uwh*]… V [CP C … (XP[wh])]] — one long leap

In (1) the wh-XP moves first to the embedded Spec-CP, then to the matrix Spec-CP; in (2) it skips the lower Spec-CP. The Ch.9 conclusion was that (1) is correct; the standard term for this is successive cyclic movement.

p.377

10.2 Empirical evidence for local movement. Three independent arguments for successive cyclicity: (i) floating quantifiers in a dialect of English; (ii) alternating complementizers in Scottish Gaelic; (iii) inversion in Spanish. 10.2.1 Floating quantifiers. West Ulster English attaches the quantifier all to a wh-word:

(3) What all did you get for Christmas?
(4) Who all did you meet when you were in Derry?
(5) Where all did they go for their holidays?

As with VP-internal subject stranding (Ch.6), this all can be stranded:

(6) What did you get all for Xmas?
(7) Who did you meet all when you were in Derry?
(8) Where did they go all for their holidays?

True of embedded questions too:

(9) I don't remember [what all I said].
(10) I don't remember [what I said all].
p.378

The constituent [what all] is Merged in θ-position; the wh-phrase can raise, stranding the quantifier (structure (11): [vP get [DP all (what)]]). For long-distance extraction, the quantifier can be stranded in the intermediate Spec-CP — exactly as predicted if movement stops there:

(12) What all did he say [(that) he wanted]?
(13) What did he say [(that) he wanted all]?
(14) What did he say [all (that) he wanted]?
(15) Where do you think [all they'll want to visit]?
(16) What do they claim [all that we did]?
(17) *What did he say [that all he wanted]?

The stranded quantifier correctly precedes that (Spec-CP is before C); (17) fails. Structure (18): [CP what … [CP (what) all C [TP … (what all)]]].

The analysis also predicts stranding in the Spec of a non-finite C, before for — correct:

(19) What were you trying all to say?
(20) What did you mean all for me to do?

This shows we must allow successive steps via Spec-CP, so "one long swoop" cannot be the only option — but it does not rule out long swoop. 10.2.2 Complementizer agreement again. In Scottish Gaelic, non-wh embedding uses the complementizer gu(n) = [C, finite]:

(21) Bha mi ag radh gun do bhuail e. ('I was saying that she hit him.')
(22) Tha mi a' smaoineachadh gu bheil Iain air a mhisg. ('I think Iain is drunk.')

(23) Co an duine a tha thu a' posadh? ('Which man are you marrying?')

Structure (25) shows a[uwh*] heading CP. Generalization (26): if Spec-CP holds a wh-phrase, a heads CP; otherwise gu does.

p.381

Under wh-extraction from an embedded clause, the expected gu is replaced by a in the embedded clause:

(27) Co an duine a bha thu ag radh a bhuail i? ('Which man were you saying she hit?')
(28) Co am bodach a tha thu a' smaoineachadh a tha air a mhisg? ('Which old man do you think is drunk?')

This entails movement stopped at the embedded Spec-CP. Crucially, keeping gu(n) under wh-movement is ungrammatical:

(29) *Co an duine a bha thu ag radh gun do bhuail i?
(30) *Co am bodach a tha thu a' smaoineachadh gu bheil air a mhisg?
p.382

So stopping at the intermediate Spec-CP is not merely an option, it is obligatory. Unlike the West Ulster data, this argument rules out one-long-swoop derivations (otherwise (29)–(30) could not be excluded). 10.2.3 Successive inversion (Spanish). Spanish (and Italian) optionally allow post-VP subjects:

(31) Maria contestó la pregunta.
(32) Contestó la pregunta Maria.

In wh-questions the inversion is obligatory:

(33) Qué querían esos dos?
(34) *Qué esos dos querían?

Generalization (35): wh-word in Spec-CP ⇒ inversion obligatory; else optional. Used as a probe for long-distance questions.

p.383

In a multi-clause declarative (36) the embedded subjects (Pedro, la revista) precede their verbs. Wh-extracting the most embedded object triggers inversion in every intermediate clause:

(37) Qué pensaba Juan que le había dicho Pedro que había publicado la revista? ('What did Juan think Pedro told him the journal had published?')
(38) *Qué pensaba Juan que Pedro le había dicho que la revista había publicado?

(37) shows inversion in main, intermediate, and most-embedded clauses; the absence in (38) is ungrammatical. So the wh-phrase must occupy each intermediate Spec-CP, ruling out one-long-swoop.

p.384

We needn't know the exact inversion mechanism — just that obligatory inversion correlates with a filled Spec-CP, extrapolating to other obligatory-inversion contexts. Again: wh-movement proceeds through Spec-CPs and cannot skip them. 10.3 Ensuring successive wh-movement. 10.3.1 Locality in feature matching. Wh-movement is triggered by a strong uninterpretable wh-feature on C; in a single clause it matches a wh-feature on a constituent within the clause:

(39) C[uwh*] … XP[wh]
p.385

Matching cannot reach into a lower clause beyond its specifier: the complement of any C is invisible to matching (40); moving XP[F] to Spec-CP makes it visible (41).

(40) C[uF] … [CP C [TP … XP[F] …]]
(41) C[uF] … [CP XP[F] C [TP … (XP[F]) …]]
(42) Feature checking under c-command reaches no further than the specifier of any embedded CP.

(42) alone won't force anything into the intermediate Spec-CP. Languages that overtly mark the intermediate position (Gaelic) wear their feature specifications on their morphological sleeves; just as English DPs bear abstract case, English embedding Cs may optionally bear an abstract [uwh*]:

(43) Embedded C may optionally bear [uwh*]. (a UG principle)

So every language has two relevant Cs (one [uwh*], one not), spelled out differently (Gaelic) or not (English).

p.386

Combining (42)+(43): if an embedded CP contains a wh-XP (44) and is embedded under a C[uwh*]-headed clause, using the plain C means the wh-feature can never be matched → the derivation crashes. Using the [uwh*] embedded C forces the wh-XP into the lower Spec-CP (45), where it becomes accessible to the higher C — forcing successive steps:

(44) C … XP[wh]
(45) XP[wh] C[uwh*] … (XP[wh])

Generalizing from CPs to DPs (and some PPs), we call such categories phases:

(46) Feature matching reaches no further than the specifier of an embedded phase.

This is the Phase Impenetrability Constraint (PIC). Chomsky derives phasehood from derivations having "short memories": material inside completed phases is forgotten (except the edge/specifier).

p.386–387

10.3.2 Solving an old puzzle. Ch.8 (8.2.6) left a puzzle with raising verbs: a finite CP embedded under seem (structure (47)). Matching T's [nom]/EPP with embedded Medea would wrongly derive:

(48) *Medea seemed that has poisoned Jason.
(49) [TP T[uD*] seemed [CP (that) Medea[D,nom] has poisoned Jason]]

PIC rules (48) out: Medea is not a wh-phrase, never reaches the lower Spec-CP, so is never accessible to matrix T's EPP.

p.387–388

But a new version arises: wh-phrases do reach intermediate Spec-CP, so why is (50) bad?

(50) *Who seemed (that) had poisoned Jason?

Derivation (51) seems possible: build TP, merge C[uwh*] raising who to Spec-CP, merge seem/v/T, match EPP raising who to matrix Spec-TP, merge matrix C. The odd step is who moving from Spec-CP to Spec-TP — traditionally called improper movement. Captured by:

(52) Improper Movement Restriction: Only wh-features are visible in the specifier of CP.

This blocks T's EPP from matching who's D-feature in Spec-CP. The restriction is admittedly a stipulation whose deeper explanation is under debate, but suffices here.

p.388–389

10.4 Island phenomena. 10.4.1 Wh-islands. With multiple wh-phrases the highest raises to matrix Spec-CP (53); the lower stays because matching targets the closest wh (superiority), ruling out (54). Same in embedded clauses:

(53) Who C (who) T … (who) saw what?
(54) *What did who … (who) see (what)?
(55) I asked who saw what.
(56) *I asked what who saw?
p.389

If one wh-phrase fills the embedded Spec-CP, a more deeply embedded one can't move higher (config (57)): the matrix C attracts the closest wh, and the deeper wh isn't at the CP-phase edge. Hence by PIC:

(57) … V [CP XP1[wh] … XP2[wh] …]
(58) *What did you ask who saw (what)?
(59) I asked who Medea gave what?
(60) *What did you ask who Medea gave (what)?
(61) It is impossible to move a wh-phrase out of a CP with another wh-phrase in its specifier.

This is the classic Wh-Island Constraint. Note: an island (descriptive: can't wh-move out of it) is not the same as a phase (theoretical: only its specifier is accessible).

p.390

D-linked wh-expressions sharpen the effect (avoiding superiority confounds). D-linking lets a low wh raise over a higher one:

(62) I asked which king invaded which city.
(63) I asked which city which king invaded.
(64) [CP which city C[uwh*] [TP which king invaded (which city)]]
(65) ?Which city did you ask which king invaded?
(66) *Which king did you ask which city invaded?

(65) (object, slightly degraded) contrasts dramatically with (66) (subject), since the embedded subject is not at the CP-phase edge — not attributable to superiority since the wh-subject is D-linked. The theory also predicts the well-formed:

(67) Who did you ask saw what?
(68) Which king did you wonder invaded which city?

So the wh-island constraint follows from the PIC.

p.391

10.4.2 DP islands. A noun with a CP, PP, or of-DP argument:

(69) I believed [DP the claim [CP that Philip would invade the city of Athens]].
(70) *Which city do you believe [DP the claim [CP that Philip would invade]]?
(71) Plato listened to [DP Demosthenes' oration [PP about Philip]].
(72) *Who did Plato listen to [DP Demosthenes' oration [PP about]]?
(73) Phaedo was interested in [DP Plato's description [of geometry]].
(74) *What was Phaedo interested in [DP Plato's description [of]]?

Traditionally Complex Noun Phrase Islands (now treated as full DPs). Captured by assuming DPs are phases: nothing inside (except Spec-DP) is accessible to a later [uwh*] C. None of these have a wh in Spec-DP.

p.392

Walking through the CP-complement case (75): even after raising which city to the embedded Spec-CP (76), embedding it in the DP the claim… (a phase) buries it in the DP's complement (77), so matrix C cannot match it.

(75) *Which city do you believe [DP the claim [CP that Philip would invade]]?
(76) [CP which city that[uwh*] [TP Philip would invade (which city)]]
(77) [CP [do C[Q,uwh*]] you believe [DP the claim [CP which city that[uwh*]…]]]
p.392–393

Could D bear [uwh*], moving a wh to Spec-DP (intermediate (79))? That requires the to host [uwh*] — but DP-internal wh-movement under the is impossible:

(78) *Which city do you believe the claim that Philip would invade?
(79) [which city] the claim that Philip would invade
(80) *Which god the statue
(81) *How fierce the battle

Same for all definite Ds, including the zero determiner that checks genitive possessors:

(82) *Which poem did you hear Homer's recital of last night?
(83) *Which poem did you hear those recitals of last night?
p.393

But indefinite Ds permit extraction:

(84) Which poem did you go to hear a recital of last night?
(85) Who did you get an accurate description of?
(86) Who did you hear an oration about?

So definite DPs are islands, indefinite ones are not. Two options: (i) phasehood depends on definiteness; (ii) indefinite Ds allow a wh-specifier. Supporting (ii), DP-internal wh-movement exists and is restricted to indefinites:

(87) a fierce battle ~ How fierce a battle.
(88) the fierce battle ~ *How fierce the battle.
(89) an oration about Philip ~ *Who an oration about

(89) shows this doesn't extend to noun arguments. Adger leaves it open, assuming only definite DPs are phases.

p.393–394

10.4.3 Subject islands. With adjective obvious (single CP argument):

(90) That Plato loved Aster was obvious.
(91) It was obvious that Plato loved Aster.
(92) Who was it obvious that Plato loved?
(93) *Who was that Plato loved obvious?

Same with CP subjects of a verb:

(94) That Plato loved Aster seemed to be known by everyone.
(95) *Who did that Plato loved seem to be known by everyone?
(96) That Plato loved Aster proved to be his undoing.
(97) *Who did that Plato loved prove to be his undoing?
p.394

Nothing — subject, prepositional object, or manner adverb — can be extracted from a CP subject:

(98) b. *Who was that loved Aster obvious?
(99) b. *Which city was that Plato lived in well known?
(100) b. *How deeply was that Plato loved Aster obvious?
(101) The Sentential Subject Island Constraint: Nothing can be moved out of a clausal subject.

Extraction from a non-initial CP argument of seem (91) is fine: build CP, raise who via C[uwh*], merge obvious (structure (102)).

p.395
(102) [AP obvious [CP who C [that Plato loved (who)]]]
(103) Who was it obvious that Plato loved?

Two analyses of sentential subjects: (a) in Spec-TP; (b) base-adjoined, with a null pronominal in Spec-TP. Under (a), moving the CP to Spec-TP (104), merging C[Q,uwh*], then raising who to matrix Spec-CP (105), the derivation converges yet is completely ungrammatical — analysis (a) fails.

(104) [TP [who that Plato loved] was obvious]
(105) [CP who C[Q,uwh*] [TP [CP (who) that Plato loved] was (CP) obvious]]
p.395–396

Under (b), the clausal subject is in the specifier of a special CP and Spec-TP holds a null pronominal (106). This can't be made a wh-question (Spec-CP is taken) nor further embedded (the special C is matrix-only). So the Sentential Subject Island facts follow from the independently motivated analysis of clausal subjects.

(106) [CP [CP (who) C that Plato loved (who)] C[Q,uwh*] [TP PRO[null] was obvious]]
p.396

But subject islands are more general — extraction from any subject is degraded:

(107) a. A programme about Euripides is on Radio 4 tonight. b. *Who is a programme about on Radio 4 tonight?
(108) a. There is a programme about Euripides on Radio 4 tonight. b. Who is there a programme about on Radio 4 tonight?

Extraction from an indefinite DP is OK (108) but degraded when the DP is in Spec-TP (107). So extraction from a Spec-TP element is degraded.

p.396–397

One approach: subjects are not in selected/θ-marked positions. Spec-TP is a non-θ position (unlike Spec-vP or complement). Hence:

(109) The specifier of a phase is only visible to feature matching if the phase is in a position where it is selected by a θ-assigning head.
p.397

For legitimate (110): the embedded CP is selected by obvious (θ-assigner), so its specifier is visible and who can move out (structure (111)).

(110) Who is it obvious that Plato loves?
(111) [… is [PredP (is) [AP obvious [CP who C [that [TP Plato loves (who)]]]]]]
p.398

If instead the embedded CP moves to Spec-TP (112), T is not a θ-assigner, so the phase's specifier is invisible and the derivation crashes. Same predicts ungrammatical extraction from a normal DP subject (113)/(114):

(112) [CP C [TP [CP who that Plato loves (who)] [T' is [PredP … obvious (who…)]]]]
(113) a. A description of Aristotle is in the book. b. *Who is a description of in the book?
(114) a. There is a description of Aristotle in the book. b. Who is there a description of in the book?

If the special-CP analysis of clausal subjects is right, extraction is ruled out twice (θ-position condition + special syntax) — an undesirable but tolerated redundancy. Three locality constraints now:

(115) 1. Feature matching reaches no further than the specifier of an embedded phase (PIC). 2. A phase's specifier is visible only if the phase is selected by a θ-assigning head. 3. Only wh-features are visible in Spec-CP (Improper Movement).
p.399

10.4.4 Adjunct islands. Temporal/causal/conditional clausal adjuncts (cf. 8.3):

(116) Hephaestus had run away, before the executioner murdered Hera.
(117) Hephaestus had run away because the executioner murdered Hera.
(118) Hephaestus would run away if the executioner murdered Hera.

Nothing can be wh-moved out:

(119) *Who had Hephaestus run away, before the executioner murdered (Hera)?
(120) *Who had Hephaestus run away, because the executioner murdered (Hera)?
(121) *Who would Hephaestus run away, if the executioner murdered (Hera)?

Predicted already: adjuncts are never θ-marked by a selecting head, so even if their C bears [uwh*], any wh in its specifier is invisible to matrix C (constraint (109)).

p.399–401

10.5 Summary. Beyond the usual intervention effect on Agree, wh-movement is further constrained. Successive cyclicity was motivated from dialectal English, Scottish Gaelic, and Spanish — wh-movement proceeds Spec-CP to Spec-CP. Captured by two principles: (122) feature checking reaches no further than the specifier of any embedded CP; (123) embedded C may optionally bear [uwh*] — both invariant UG principles. This solved the Ch.8 raising puzzle ((122)) and its new variant (improper movement, (124): only wh-features visible in Spec-CP). Generalizing CP to phase gives the PIC (125), unifying wh-islands and (some) DP-islands; adding (126) that a phase must be in a θ-position for its specifier to be accessible covers subject and adjunct islands.

(122) Feature checking under c-command reaches no further than the specifier of any embedded CP.
(123) Embedded C may optionally bear [uwh*].
(124) Improper Movement Restriction: Only wh-features are visible in Spec-CP.
(125) Phase Impenetrability Constraint: Feature checking under c-command reaches no further than the specifier of any embedded phase.
(126) A phase must be in a θ-position if its specifier is to be accessible for feature matching.

Adger candidly notes this chapter's theory is more embryonic: results are largely stipulated (PIC, Improper Movement, the θ-position condition) rather than falling out of deeper architecture — though PIC at least is independently motivated by successive-cyclicity arguments and then independently gets wh-islands and some DP-islands right. The empirical base of island effects (how much is parsing vs syntax) and cross-linguistic island variation are barely touched: much work remains.
