本章把第 3 章引入的合併操作 Merge 落實成一套完整的詞組結構表徵。核心主張是：句子的階層結構並非任意，而是由中心語（head）的選擇性質（selectional properties）驅動而成。我們會看到三個結構位置——補語（complement）、指定語（specifier）、附加語（adjunct）——如何從 Merge 的先後與特徵核檢自然導出，並由此重建傳統的 X-bar 理論。接著引入新的句法關係 c-command（成分統御） 作為診斷工具，用它分析雙賓結構，導出小寫 v（little v）與 VP-shell（動詞殼層）分析；最後以 UTAH（題旨指派一致性假設）與整個系統的推導（derivation）架構收尾。

本章的核心問題意識

第 3 章告訴我們「句子是 Merge 一層層蓋起來的階層結構」。但這還不夠：我們需要知道這些階層如何組織成「詞組（phrase）」、為什麼某個位置叫補語、某個叫指定語、又如何用獨立的證據（c-command、構成性測試、移位測試）去判定一個句子的真實結構。Adger 的關鍵立場是：詞組層級（XP / X′ / X）不是理論硬塞進去的原始概念，而是「特徵核檢」這件事的副產品——一個節點若再無待核檢的選擇特徵，它就自動是 maximal（最大投射，XP）。

4.2 詞組的結構

書頁 104–105

句子是用二元操作 Merge 把句法物件結合而成。中心語的性質會「投射（project）」到更高的結構，使得結構中最重要的元素決定了整個結構的分布。本節聚焦：中心語的性質究竟能往上投射多遠。

4.2.1 第一次 Merge — 補語

系統的一個重要預測：一個元素的所有 c-選擇特徵（c-selectional features）必須在它與一個「選擇它的中心語」合併之前就被核檢掉。這正排除了 (1)：

(1) * letters to 介詞 to 帶有未被核檢的 c-選擇特徵 [uN]（需要一個名詞補語）。由於特徵只能在 Merge 時、且 Merge 永遠發生在根節點（root node）核檢，這個 [uN] 被「困（trapped）」在結構內部，永遠無法核檢 → 不合語法。
(2) ✓ letters to Peter 先把名詞 Peter 與 to 合併，核檢掉 [uN]，就合語法了。to Peter 這個成分已無待核檢的選擇特徵，無法再投射，稱為 maximal（最大投射）。範疇為 X 的最大物件縮寫為 XP，即「X 詞組」。
         N(letters)
        ╱        ╲
  letters[N,…]    PP
              ╱      ╲
        to[P,uN]   Peter[N]

注意：作者不在根節點標 XP（因為還不知道 letters 是否會再被選擇，特徵束裡留了「…」）；也不把終端的 Peter 標成 phrase——雖然技術上 Peter 既是 maximal（無選擇特徵）也是 minimal（它就是個詞項）。

書頁 106
節點Minimal?Maximal?說明
Peter是是詞項，且無 c-選擇特徵
to是否詞項，但帶一個待核檢特徵
to Peter否是非詞項，且無待核檢特徵
letters是否詞項，帶待核檢特徵
letters to Peter否?非詞項；可能仍有待核檢特徵（故 maximal 與否未定）
關鍵原則：一個節點的詞組地位（phrasal status）是從它的特徵構成「導出」的——若無 c-選擇特徵待核檢，它就是 maximal。由此得出：只有最大投射能當「選擇性中心語」的姊妹；若把非最大投射合併進去，其未核檢特徵會被困住而導致不合語法。

以動詞 burn 為例（兩個 θ-角色、各帶一個 c-選擇 N-特徵）。先處理 Theme，把 letters 的投射與 burn 合併：

書頁 107 
          V(burn)
        ╱        ╲
  burn[V,uN,…]    NP(letters to Peter)
                ╱        ╲
          letters         PP
                       ╱      ╲
                 to[P,uN]   Peter[N]

合併後 letters to Peter 的所有選擇特徵都已核檢，故此刻變成 phrasal（NP），並滿足 burn 的 c-選擇特徵。動詞的投射暫不標 phrasal（要等它再合併別的東西才確定）。構成性測試（替代測試）證明這結構正確：

(6) Burn letters to Peter!
(7) Burn letters to him!
(8) Burn them! 用代詞 him 替換 Peter、用 them 替換整個 letters to Peter 都成立 → 證明 letters to Peter 是一個構成成分（constituent）。這種「中心語 + 投射的複雜詞組」結構稱為 中心語-補語結構（head-complement structure），由 Merge 的第一次應用（滿足中心語第一個選擇特徵）產生。

術語：(5) 中 NP letters to Peter 是 burn 的補語；PP to Peter 是 letters 的補語；NP Peter 是 to 的補語。英語裡補語在中心語右側（法、蓋爾、阿拉伯語亦然）；日、韓、土耳其語則補語在中心語左側。

(9) Hanako ga Taro o tataku（日語） Hanako 主格　Taro 賓格　打　／「Hanako 正在打 Taro。」 補語 Taro（受詞）在英語中跟在動詞後，在日語中卻在動詞前。
(10) buturigaku no gakusei（日語） 物理　屬格　學生　／「物理學的學生」 — 名詞的補語也在左側。
(11) Michael left Meg. 動詞補語的傳統名稱是受詞（object）；Meg 既是 met 的受詞也是補語。受詞在動詞後 = VO 語言；受詞在動詞前 = OV 語言。

當中心語 X 只與單一補語結合時，基本中心語-補語結構有兩種線性化：

(12) [XP X YP]（中心語在前，如英語）
(13) [XP YP X]（中心語在後，如日語） 本書假設不同詞序源自中心語-補語結構不同的線性化性質；其他分析（如 Kayne 的「皆源自移位」）見延伸閱讀。

4.2.2 第二次 Merge — 指定語

書頁 109

burn 的 θ-grid 上還有第二個元素：施事（Agent）角色。補語合併後，動詞仍有一個未滿足的選擇特徵，需再合併一個名詞當施事：

(14) Paul burns letters to Peter. Paul 得到 Agent、letters to Peter 得到 Theme。
           VP
        ╱       ╲
   NP(Paul)      V′
              ╱      ╲
   burn[V,uN,uN]      NP(letters to Peter)

動詞的一個 c-選擇特徵未核檢，會投射到上一個節點，在那裡由作為姊妹的 NP Paul 核檢。這給出動詞的三個投射層級：

三層投射（皆由特徵核檢「導出」，而非額外原始概念）：
① 最小投射 minimal projection（Xmin，此處 V）：就是詞項本身，其實不算「投射」。
② 最大投射 maximal projection（XP，即 VP）：所有選擇特徵核檢完畢時產生。
③ 中間投射 intermediate / bar 投射（X′，即 V′）：非詞項，但仍有待核檢特徵。

定義：補語＝詞項的姊妹（第一次 Merge 的產物）；指定語（specifier）＝ X′ 投射的姊妹（第二次 Merge 的產物）。英語指定語線性化在中心語左側。某些語言（如 Malagasy，例 (16)）的指定語似乎在右側，但這分析有爭議。

(16) Manasa lamba ho an'ny ankizy ny lehilahy（Malagasy） 現在式-洗　衣服　為　兒童　那　男人　／「那男人為小孩洗衣服。」（施事看似在右）

4.2.3 附加（Adjunction）

書頁 110–112

有些成分不接受中心語指派的 θ-角色，而是修飾整個情境：

(17) Anson demonized David every day.
(18) Anson demonized David at the club.（PP 附加語）
(19) Anson demonized David almost constantly.（AdvP 附加語）
(20) Anson very happily demonized David. demonize 把兩個 θ-角色指派給 Anson 與 David；而 every day / at the club / constantly / happily 不接受任何 θ-角色，純為修飾。這類成分稱 附加語（adjuncts）——它們被納入句子，但不是透過選擇特徵核檢。附加語不是範疇名稱，而是「樹中佔據某種位置」的句法物件（可為 AP、PP、AdvP 等）。

三者的平行性（皆為「姊妹」關係）

本書採保守立場：補語是詞項（X）的姊妹；指定語是 X′ 節點的姊妹；附加語是 XP 節點的姊妹。例 (21)：

              XP
           ╱      ╲
         XP      Adjunct
        ╱   ╲
   Specifier   X′
            ╱     ╲
           X    Complement

附加時 XP 的詞組層級不變，因為沒有滿足任何選擇特徵。

為什麼附加語能進入樹？Merge 須由選擇特徵觸發，但這裡沒有特徵被滿足；又不能把選擇特徵放在附加語上（否則它就成了中心語）。證據顯示附加語從不是其構造的中心語：帶附加語的成分，其分布與沒有附加語時完全相同（例 (22) (quickly) 加不加，VP 的分布不變）。

(22) a. Burn the letters (quickly)!　b. I burnt the letters (quickly).　c. I plan to burn the letters (quickly).　d. *Burn the letters (quickly) is the best thing to do.　e. Burning the letters (quickly) is the best thing to do. 加不加 quickly，可接受性完全一樣 → 附加語不改變 VP 的分布。

因此附加語不是由 Merge 納入，而由另一基本操作 Adjoin（附加）——它不需要觸發，把一個詞組物件插入另一個詞組物件的最外層，不創造新物件，只把舊物件最外層「撐開」兩半再插入：

(23) [XP XP YP]（YP 附加到 XP；英語附加語不線性化，可在兩側）
(24) Kiss Anson quickly! / (26) Quickly kiss Anson! 祈使句有不發音的施事（標為 e）。(25)/(27) 顯示 quickly 可附加在 VP 右側或左側：[VP [VP e [V′ kiss Anson]] quickly] 或 [VP quickly [VP e [V′ kiss Anson]]]。

附加元素的性質：永遠在補語與指定語的階層外側、可在兩側出現、不接受 θ-角色、（經規定）為 phrasal。一個遺留問題（留待第 5 章）：

(28) Julie quickly answered the question. 副詞夾在主語與動詞之間，但若主語在 VP 指定語、附加語又必在指定語外側，這就矛盾了。此問題下一章解決。

4.2.4 動詞詞組的結構

書頁 113–114

及物動詞詞組的概要結構（例 (29)）：動詞先與補語合併成 V′，再與指定語合併成 VP，附加語可再附加到 VP。指定語與補語都必須是 phrasal（無 c-選擇特徵）。

              VP
           ╱      ╲
         VP      Adjunct
        ╱   ╲
   Specifier   V′
            ╱     ╲
         verb    Complement

系統允許一個詞項帶多個選擇特徵，這引出核檢順序問題。為什麼重要？因為語意要正確。(30) Jenny poked Jonathan 沒有歧義（誰戳誰是固定的）；但若先核檢「錯的」特徵，θ-角色會指派反了（變成 Jonathan 戳 Jenny）。此問題留待 4.5 解決。

4.2.5 X-bar 理論

書頁 115–116

我們發展出的系統，大致就是傳統的 X-bar 理論（X-bar theory）。它原本被設想成對詞組結構的一組約束：所有詞組都必須有中心語；兩個關鍵結構是中心語-補語、指定語-X。常見格式：

(31) XP → ZP X′
(32) X′ → X WP
(33) [XP ZP [X′ X WP]]，ZP = 指定語，WP = 補語。

傳統 X-bar 樹與本書的差異有二：(1) 傳統 X-bar 把詞項支配於最小投射層之下（而非「就是」那層）；(2) 傳統 X-bar 強制每一層都投射——即使沒有指定語或補語，每個 XP 都直接支配 X′、X′ 支配 X、X 支配詞項。例 (15) 在傳統 X-bar 下會變成 (34) 那種層層填滿、極為冗長的樹（每個 NP 都展開成 N′、N）。

本書 vs 傳統 X-bar 的根本差異：本書的詞組層級由詞項性質導出，所以 bar 投射（X′）只有在「有指定語」時才出現。若中心語只與補語結合且特徵都滿足，補語自動 phrasal；若中心語既無補語也無指定語，它自動同時是 maximal 與 minimal（如專有名詞、代詞）。傳統 X-bar 樹更整齊統一，但多出大量冗餘結構層。

4.3 C-command（成分統御）

書頁 116–117

除了「姊妹關係（sisterhood）」外，引入另一個重要句法關係 c-command（constituent-command，成分統御）。它能診斷結構，並在反身代詞、否定極性詞的分析中扮演關鍵角色，最後用來在雙賓結構的多個假設間做抉擇。

4.3.1 定義關係

(35) 節點 A c-command 節點 B，當且僅當（iff） A 的姊妹：a. 就是 B，或 b. 包含（contains）B。

「當且僅當」＝兩條件任一成立 A 就 c-command B，反之 A c-command B 則必有一條件成立。第二條件可想成指令：「想知道某節點是否 c-command 另一節點，就看它的姊妹是否包含後者」。

(36) [T [Z X Y] [S W R]] X 只 c-command Y（Y 是 X 姊妹，且 Y 不含其他節點）。Z c-command S（姊妹），也 c-command W 與 R（含於 S）。S c-command Z、X、Y（定義不涉及線性順序，故可統御「前面」的節點）。T 既不 c-command 任何東西、也不被任何東西 c-command（它含全樹、無姊妹）。

4.3.2 反身代詞約束

書頁 117–119
(37) ✓ I shaved myself.
(38) * Myself shaved me. 第 2 章的「反身代詞概括」（(39)：反身代詞須與句中另一表達式同指；(40)：同指須共享 φ-特徵）無法區分 (37)/(38)——兩句都有共享 φ-特徵的反身代詞。
(41) 反身代詞概括（修訂）：反身代詞必須與一個 c-command 它的表達式同指。 主語 c-command 受詞（受詞含於主語姊妹 V′ 內），但反之不然。(38) 中反身代詞 Myself 在主語位置，me 不 c-command 它 → 排除。例 (42) [VP myself [V′ shaved me]] 顯示 me 不 c-command myself。

能否改用線性「前置（precedence）」取代 c-command？(43) 用「須與前置表達式同指」也能解釋 (37)/(38)，但無法推廣：

(44) The man I saw left.
(45) * The man I saw shaved myself. I 雖前置於 myself，句子卻不合法。因 the man I saw 是構成成分（(46)–(49) 的替代/分裂測試證明），I 被包在裡面，所以 I 不 c-command myself（I 上方有個不含反身代詞的節點）。結構 (51) [VP [NP The man I saw] [V′ shaved myself]]。→ 我們需要的是 c-command 而非 precedence。
(52) * My mother hated myself. 所有格 my 在主語 my mother 內部，不 c-command 反身代詞 → 違反概括，同樣排除。

4.3.3 否定極性詞 NPI

書頁 120–121

否定極性詞（Negative Polarity Items, NPIs）如 any、ever，需要句中出現否定元素：

(53) * I wanted any cake. / (54) I didn't want any cake.
(55) * I saw him ever. / (56) I didn't see him ever.
(57) NPI 概括：NPI 必須出現在否定句中。
(58) ✓ No one wanted any cake.
(59) * Any boy saw no one. (58)(59) 都是「含 no one 的否定句 + any」，但只有 (58) 合法 → 純「否定句」概括不夠。
(60) NPI 概括（修訂）：NPI 必須被否定元素 c-command。 (59) 中 any boy 在主語位置，不被 no one（受詞）c-command → 排除。再驗證 (61) *The picture of no one hung upon any wall：no one 在構成成分 the picture of no one 內部（(62) 替代測試證明），不 c-command any → 排除。關鍵：相關的是結構概念（c-command），而非純線性（precedence）或純語意（negativity）。

4.3.4 小結

書頁 122

c-command 同時解釋反身代詞與 NPI 的分布（練習中還會解釋代詞與其他 NP）。若解釋正確，c-command 就成了診斷句子結構的工具：把反身代詞或 NPI 放進感興趣的位置，用合語法性模式檢測 c-command 關係。這為句法工具箱（除構成性測試外）增添新利器，下一節用它分析雙賓動詞詞組。

4.4 雙賓結構（Ditransitives）

書頁 122–123

到目前為止「補語（第一次 Merge）+ 指定語（第二次 Merge）」的及物結構，遇到三論元述語就出問題。

4.4.1 三論元述語

(63) Benjamin gave the cloak to Nathan.
(64) Nathan received the cloak from Benjamin. give、receive（如同上章的 donate）都是三位述語（three-place predicates），事件中有三個參與者，各 NP/PP 由述語指派 θ-角色。

4.4.2 三分枝還是二分枝？

書頁 123–126

三位述語的構成性問題：雙賓 VP 怎麼結構？兩個分析——三分枝 vs 二分枝。

(65) [V′ gave [NP the cloak] [PP to Nathan]]（三分枝） 但若只保留二元 Merge，這結構不可得——須引入能造三元結構的操作。
(66) [V′ [V′ gave[V,uN,uN,uP] [NP the cloak]] [PP to Nathan]]（V-遞迴的二分枝） give 帶三特徵 [uN, uN, uP]：一個 N 由受詞核檢、另一個由主語、P 由 Goal 核檢。此分析涉及 V 層級的遞迴（先前只在附加語見過遞迴，但 Goal 不像附加語，它有 θ-角色，所以有點令人不安）。

核檢順序此時更迫切：若 P 比 N 先核檢，PP 會成為動詞姊妹，預測錯誤詞序 (67) ?Benjamin gave to Lee the cloak（除非特殊語調，假設是受詞右移）。代詞更明顯：(68) a.*Benjamin gave to Lee it　b. Benjamin gave it to Lee。

三分枝 vs V-遞迴對構成性主張不同：V-遞迴下「動詞+受詞」是構成成分，三分枝則否。哪個對？移位測試（VP-Preposing）：

(69) Benjamin said he would run away and [run away] he did.
(71) ✓ …and [give the cloak to Lee] he did.（動詞+受詞+Goal 可前移）
(72) * …and [give the cloak] he did to Lee.（只前移動詞+受詞 → 不行） 二分枝把 give the cloak 當成分，理應可前移，但 (72) 不行 → 沒有證據支持「動詞+受詞」是成分。
(73) a. Who gave the cloak to Lee?　b. *Benjamin (did) to Lee.（替代/省略測試同樣失敗）

並列（co-ordination）測試：(74)–(77) 顯示 and 可連接兩 NP、兩 NP、兩動詞投射、兩 AP；(78)(79) 非成分不能並列。但並列須小心：(80) [Lee's youngest] and [Dawn's oldest] son ran away 看似連接非成分，實則是 (81) 名詞 son 被省略的構成成分並列（特殊語調為證）。回到雙賓：(82) Benjamin [gave the cloak] and [sent the book] to Lee 處於可接受邊緣，且須長停頓 → (83) 其實是 gave the cloak 0（刪略）。

4.4.2 結論：沒有任何構成性證據支持「動詞與受詞（排除 PP）構成一個成分」。這似乎傾向三分枝——即便我們只採二元 Merge。但下一節會發現另有證據支持另一種二分枝模式：受詞與 PP 構成一個單位。

4.4.3 二分枝替代方案

(84) Sam gave [the cloak to Lee] and [the magic chalice to Matthew]. 並列「受詞+Goal」且無特殊語調停頓（不像 (82)）→ 暗示 the cloak to Lee 是真正的構成成分。給出 (86) 的結構：PP 是某中心語（暫標「?」）的補語，受詞在其指定語，整個 ?P 是動詞的補語。
         V′
       ╱    ╲
   gave[V]   ?P
          ╱     ╲
   NP(the cloak)  ?′
               ╱    ╲
              ?      PP(to Lee)

此結構與「無動詞-受詞成分」的事實相容（不主張動詞+受詞成分）。但移位無證據支持 ?P 存在（(87) *…and [the cloak to Lee] he gave）——不過可能有獨立因素排除它（VP-preposing 對範疇敏感）。三個假設並陳：三分枝、Goal 右附加 V 的二分枝、含 ?P 的二分枝。唯一與並列證據一致的是第三個（?P）。

用 c-command 進一步抉擇：雙賓構式（double object construction）＝缺介詞的雙賓：

(88) a. Benjamin gave Lee the cloak.　b. Calum sent Nathan the binoculars.　c. Lee showed Benjamin the unicorn. 無介詞的 Goal 稱間接受詞（indirect object）；各有對應介詞句 (89)。並列 (90) 也支持「間接+直接受詞」成構。
(91) * Emily showed himself Benjamin in the mirror.
(92) ✓ Emily showed Benjamin himself in the mirror. 反身代詞約束的不對稱！要解釋這個，三分枝 (93) 與「右附加」(94) 都錯誤地預測 (91) 合法（因 Benjamin c-command himself）；只有 ?P 二分枝 (95) 正確：[V′ show [?P himself [?′ ? Benjamin]]] 中 Benjamin 不 c-command himself → 正確排除 (91)。

帶介詞 Goal 的簡單雙賓無法做同樣論證（介詞永遠阻擋 c-command，(96)–(98) 在三分枝/二分枝下都排除）。只有雙賓構式能用此法。最終為簡單雙賓也採 ?P 結構 (99)：[V′ show [?P Benjamin [?′ ? [PP to himself]]]]。

4.4.3 結論：雖然構成性（並列）證據較弱，但用反身代詞的 c-command 不對稱，可確定 VP 內部的 c-command 關係，從而判定 ?P 結構優於三分枝。

4.4.4 使役與小寫 v（little v）

書頁 131–136

那個標「?」的中心語到底是什麼？採用的假設：它模擬一種語意關係——使役（causality）。許多語言有使役結構（causatives），英語也有類似的：

(100) a. Emily caused Benjamin to see himself in the mirror.　b. Benjamin caused Lee to have the cloak.　c. Benjamin caused the book to go to Ross.
(101) a. Emily showed Benjamin himself in the mirror.　b. Benjamin gave Lee the cloak.　c. Benjamin sent the book to Ross. (100) 的 cause 改寫，幾乎就是 (101) 簡單雙賓的釋義 → 直覺：雙賓的底層結構很像「使役」結構。(102) 顯示透過「刪 to → 移動 see 到 caused 旁 → caused-see 換成 showed」就能從使役導出雙賓。

其他語言更清楚地顯示這些操作。法語使役中動詞前移到主語前：

(103) Georges mange.（「Georges 吃。」主語在動詞前）
(104) Pascale fait manger Georges.（使役中動詞 manger 在主語 Georges 前）
(105) Pascale fait manger Georges <manger>. 出現 移位（Movement）操作：把 Merge 形成的結構中某元素移到另一位置；留下的位置用尖括號 <…> 標記為 語跡（trace）。
(106)/(107) Chichewa（班圖語）：-chit-its-（do-cause）與動詞 -gw-（fall）並現；(107) 動詞 -gw- 移入較高複合，構成形態複雜的「make-fall」。

小寫 v、VP-shell、語跡

採用假設：「?」其實是動詞的投射，會移入一個編碼「使役」的較高位置。這個新使役範疇記為 小寫 v（little v）。show 的底層意義是「see」，但須移入意義為「cause」的動詞。移位前後 (108)/(109)：

(108) 移位前              (109) 移位後
      vP                      vP
     ╱  ╲                    ╱   ╲
    v    VP            v+show     VP
        ╱  ╲                    ╱   ╲
   himself  V′           himself    V′
          ╱   ╲                  ╱     ╲
       show   Benjamin     <show>   Benjamin

那個移入較高位置的使役動詞稱 輕動詞（light verb），整套分析叫 VP-shell（VP 殼層）分析。奇特後果：標 VP 的成分裡沒有外顯動詞，只有移走範疇的「鬼影」——即語跡（traces）（用尖括號標記），不發音、不餵養形態/音韻規則。

(110) 同樣分析延伸到 PP-Goal。選擇特徵如何運作？show 標為 [V, uN, uP]，little v 標為 [v, uN]。(111) 顯示在姊妹關係下核檢：V 先以 PP 核檢 [uP]，未核檢的 [uN] 投射到 V′、由 V 的指定語 NP 核檢；little v 與整個 VP 合併並投射其 [uN] 到 v，再由施事 NP 核檢。

投射層級（Hierarchy of Projections）：怎麼確保 little v 一定投射？不說「v c-選擇 VP」（因為 v 不對 VP 指派 θ-角色，與「選擇＝θ」的緊密關係不符）。改用一個獨立的投射層級：(112) v > V——只要有 little v，它必有一個 VP 補語。直觀上 vP 是 VP 投射的延伸（仍是動詞性、但加入更多語意）。違反層級的結構（如 (113) 直接 [vP v NP]）不被生成。

此分析的好處：維持單一版本的 Merge（只允許二分枝）、把雙賓結構與一般使役結構連結起來。代價：除 Merge 外多了新操作 Move（移位）。理論上「二元 Merge + Move」是否優於「二元+三元 Merge」並不顯然，但本書採 Merge+Move 路線並探索其後果（第 5、6、7 章會看到更多移位證據，移位將變得無所不在）。結論：雙賓比表面複雜得多——受詞與 PP 含於單一成分內，且前者 c-command 後者。

4.5 連結（Linking）與 UTAH

書頁 136–138

回到 4.2.4 的遺留問題：如何約束核檢順序，使句法-語意介面正確？以 show（兩選擇特徵）為例，(114) 是正確核檢，(115) 是「反過來」核檢，後者會生成不合法的 (116) *Nathan showed to Benjamin it。由於 PP 比受詞更靠近動詞的句子只能靠（代詞無法使用的）移位產生，(116) 唯一可能的剖析是論元都在基底位置 → 證明受詞的基底位置是動詞的指定語，而非補語。

連結問題（Linking Problem）與 UTAH

VP-shell 分析把三位述語的施事放在 little v 指定語、Theme 放在 VP 指定語；但及物分析把施事放 VP 指定語、Theme 放 V 補語。兩者相容，卻意味同一個 θ-角色（Theme）沒有統一的句法表徵（時而補語、時而指定語）。這讓兒童得逐一學習「哪個述語配哪種結構」——此即 連結問題（Linking Problem）。若每個 θ-角色都對應固定句法配置，兒童就能從 θ-角色預測句法結構。據此提出：

(117) UTAH（題旨指派一致性假設，Uniformity of Theta Assignment Hypothesis）：述語與其論元之間相同的題旨關係，在 Merge 時由相同的結構關係句法表徵。

UTAH 是引導性假設（可能被推翻，如二分枝假設），功能是排除一整類分析；若它排除的分析確實較差，它就獲支持。

假設 UTAH，如何讓兩個分析相容？讓所有施事都在 little v 指定語。簡單及物動詞 burn 也變成 (118)：

(118)         vP
           ╱      ╲
       Agent       v′
                ╱      ╲
               v        VP
                     ╱      ╲
            burn[V,uN]      Theme

可粗略釋義為 (119) 「X causes Y to burn」。施事是 vP 指定語；Theme 在及物時是補語、三位述語時是指定語——但兩者都是 VP 的女兒（daughter）。再把 Goal 定義為 V 的女兒。於是各 θ-角色對應唯一句法配置：

(120) a. NP 為 vP 的女兒 → 詮釋為 Agent　b. NP 為 VP 的女兒 → 詮釋為 Theme　c. PP 為 V′ 的女兒 → 詮釋為 Goal

UTAH 也解答核檢順序：若特徵「反過來」核檢（如 (121)＝重複的 (115)），UTAH 被違反——PP 不是 V′ 女兒就無法詮釋。

UTAH 對非作格（unergative）與非賓格（unaccusative）述語做出明確預測：非作格（run, laugh, jump）單一論元是施事 → 在 vP 女兒 (122)；非賓格（fall, collapse, wilt）單一論元是 Theme → 在 VP 女兒 (123)。

(122) 非作格 laugh          (123) 非賓格 fall
        vP                         vP
       ╱  ╲                       ╱   ╲
   Agent   v′                    v     VP
          ╱  ╲                        ╱   ╲
         v   laugh[V]          fall[V,uN]  Theme

(122) 約略意為「X 是某笑事件的起因」；(123) 約略意為「X 經歷一個無起因的墜落事件」（這裡「cause」用得很抽象，只標示事件是否涉及施事）。本書假設非賓格也有 vP，只是其 v 語意上非使役、無施事指定語（為維持投射層級）。

義大利語提供證據：telefonare「打電話」是非作格（單一論元為施事），arrivare「抵達」是非賓格（單一論元為 Theme）。最著名的差異是助動詞選擇：

(124) Molte ragazze telefonano.「許多女孩在打電話。」
(125) Molte ragazze arrivano.「許多女孩在抵達。」
(126) Molte ragazze hanno telefonato.（非作格用 have 類助動詞，過去分詞不與主語一致）
(127) Molte ragazze sono arrivate.（非賓格用 be 類助動詞，過去分詞與主語一致） 完成式中兩類動詞用不同助動詞、過去分詞一致模式不同——正是 UTAH 所預測：非賓格與非作格因唯一論元的句法位置不同而展現句法差異（第 6 章續論）。

4.6 系統的整體架構

書頁 141–146

整個方法依賴 推導（derivation）：把句法操作（Merge、Adjoin、Move）相繼應用到句法物件上，形成越來越大的物件。每次操作推進推導一步；當無法再應用任何操作時推導停止，最終物件由心智的概念/語意系統詮釋。一個句子的推導包含許多子推導（分別構造各成分）。

推導的輸入是詞項（含音韻、語意、句法特徵）；一組詞項稱 編號集（numeration）。第一步是從編號集 Select（選取）一個元素；因單一詞項無法應用操作，再 Select 第二個，然後 Merge/Adjoin 成新物件。抽象範例 (128)–(130)：

(128) a. 步驟1 Select A　b. 步驟2 Select B
(129) 步驟3 Merge A、B → [A A B]；再 Select C、D，步驟6 Merge C、D → [C C D]
(130) 步驟7 Adjoin 步驟3 與步驟6 的輸出。
收斂 vs 崩潰：當推導順利終止、所有不可詮釋特徵都被核檢，稱為 收斂（converge）；若終止但仍有未核檢的不可詮釋特徵，則 崩潰（crash）（借電腦程式之喻）。

具體範例：編號集 {saw, v, Sam, Benjamin}。

步驟1 Select saw、Benjamin，Merge，核檢 saw 的 uN：
        VP
       ╱  ╲
  saw[V,uN]  Benjamin[N]

步驟2 Select v，與步驟1輸出 Merge（遵守投射層級）；
      v 的 c-選擇特徵 [uN] 投射到 v 母節點：
         v′
       ╱    ╲
   v[uN]     VP
          ╱     ╲
   saw[V,uN]   Benjamin

步驟3 Move saw 到 v（此移位是強制的）：
         v′[uN]
       ╱      ╲
   v+saw       VP
            ╱     ╲
       <saw>    Benjamin

步驟4 Select Sam，與步驟3輸出 Merge，核檢 v 的 uN：
            vP
          ╱    ╲
     Sam[N]     v′
             ╱     ╲
         v+saw      VP
                 ╱     ╲
             <saw>   Benjamin

Merge 很單純（取詞項或先前輸出為輸入）；Move 較複雜：它鎖定先前已建好的樹中某部分（如詞項 saw），複製該項，再與樹的另一部分（little v）Merge，並留下被抽取項的語跡。此例是詞項移位；後續會看到詞組移位。

兩個介面層：LF 與 PF

推導的某個句法物件與心智中管「意義」的部分介接——稱 概念-意圖系統（Conceptual-Intentional, CI）；這個物件稱 邏輯形式（Logical Form, LF），是一個介面層（Interface Level）（通常即終端物件）。另一邊與管「物理實現（聲音/手勢）」的 發音-知覺系統（Articulatory-Perceptual, AP）介接；但介接 AP 的不是純句法物件，而是某句法物件作為額外（音韻/形態）操作的輸入——這個物件稱 拼讀點（point of Spellout），產出的表徵稱 語音形式（Phonetic Form, PF）。要分清「拼讀點（仍是樹）」與「PF（與外部系統介接的層）」。

整體架構：編號集（取自詞庫）→ Select 把詞項放上「句法桌面（workspace）」→ Merge / Move / Adjoin 遞迴應用 → 一個物件介接 CI（LF），另一個介接 AP（拼讀點）。本系統假設句法是高度推導性的；其他理論則主張無推導、而是「互相作用的約束集」——學界仍在辯論孰優。

4.2 The structure of phrases. Sentences are built by binary Merge; the selecting head's properties project upward, so the most important element determines the structure's distribution. The section asks how far a head's properties project. 4.2.1 First Merge — Complements. All c-selectional features must be checked before an element Merges with a c-selecting head; Merge always applies at the root node.

(1) *letters to
(2) letters to Peter

(1) is out: to's [uN] feature is "trapped" and can never be checked. In (2), merging a noun first checks it. Objects with no c-selectional features left (e.g. to Peter) are maximal — they project no further. A maximal object of category X is abbreviated XP, a phrase of category X.

p.106

A node's phrasal status is derived from its featural composition: no c-selectional features left ⇒ maximal. A table classifies each node of letters to Peter as minimal/maximal. It follows that only maximal projections can be sisters of c-selecting heads; merging a non-maximal projection traps unchecked features ⇒ ungrammaticality. Example head burn: two θ-roles, each with a c-selectional N-feature.

p.107

Merging the projection of letters (now phrasal, all features checked) with burn gives a head-complement structure — the product of the first Merge satisfying a head's selectional feature. The replacement test confirms constituency:

(6) Burn letters to Peter!
(7) Burn letters to him!
(8) Burn them!

NP letters to Peter is the complement of burn; to Peter of letters; Peter of to. English complements follow their head (also French, Gaelic, Arabic); Japanese, Korean, Turkish put complements first.

p.108
(9) Hanako ga Taro o tataku ('Hanako is hitting Taro') — complement precedes verb
(10) buturigaku no gakusei ('the student of physics')
(11) Michael left Meg.

A verb's complement is traditionally the object. Object-after-verb = VO languages; object-before-verb = OV. For a single complement, two linearizations:

(12) [XP X YP]　(13) [XP YP X]

The book attributes the orders to different linearization properties of head-complement structures (alternatives in Further Reading).

p.109

4.2.2 Second Merge — Specifiers. burn's second θ-grid element is the Agent. After the complement merges, an unsatisfied selectional feature remains; another nominal merges to check it and is assigned Agent.

(14) Paul burns letters to Peter.

The unchecked feature projects to the next node up, checked by sister NP Paul. Three projection levels emerge: minimal (Xmin, the lexical item), maximal (XP, all features checked), and intermediate / bar-level (X′, not lexical but with features still to check).

p.110

Crucially, projection levels are derived from feature checking, not extra primitives. A complement = sister of a lexical item (first Merge); a specifier = sister of an X′ projection (second Merge). English specifiers linearize to the left of the head. Malagasy seems to put the Agent on the right (controversial):

(16) Manasa lamba ho an'ny ankizy ny lehilahy ('The man is washing clothes for the children').
p.111

4.2.3 Adjunction. Some constituents take a modificational, not argumental, role and receive no θ-role:

(17) Anson demonized David every day.
(18) …at the club.　(19) …almost constantly.　(20) Anson very happily demonized David.

These are adjuncts — incorporated without checking selectional features. "Adjunct" names a structural position, not a category (AP, PP, AdvP, etc.). The book's conservative position: complements are sisters of lexical items, specifiers sisters of X′, adjuncts sisters of XP (21). Adjoining an XP does not change the XP level.

p.112

Adjuncts cannot be triggered by a selectional feature (that would make them heads), and the evidence is they are never heads: a constituent with an adjunct distributes exactly as without it.

(22) a. Burn the letters (quickly)! … d. *Burn the letters (quickly) is the best thing to do. e. Burning the letters (quickly) is the best thing to do.

So adjuncts enter via a separate, untriggered operation, Adjoin, which inserts a phrasal object at the outermost level of another, stretching its top layer:

(23) [XP XP YP]

English adjunction is not linearized (adjuncts on either side).

p.113

Adjoined elements: always hierarchically outside complements/specifiers, can appear on either side, get no θ-role, and (by stipulation) are phrasal.

(24) Kiss Anson quickly!　(26) Quickly kiss Anson!

Imperatives have an unpronounced Agent (notated e) in Spec-VP; (25)/(27) show quickly adjoined on the right or left of VP.

p.114
(28) Julie quickly answered the question.

A problem (deferred to Ch.5): the adverb comes between subject and verb, yet adjuncts should be external to specifiers. 4.2.4 The structure of the verb phrase. A transitive VP (29): verb + complement ⇒ V′; + specifier ⇒ VP; adjuncts adjoin to VP. Specifiers and complements must be phrasal. With multiple selectional features the order of checking matters for semantics.

p.115
(30) Jenny poked Jonathan.

No ambiguity about agent/theme; checking the wrong feature first would reverse θ-roles (addressed in 4.5). 4.2.5 X-bar Theory. The system reconstructs much of traditional X-bar theory — constraints requiring all phrases to have heads, the two key types being head-complement and specifier-X:

(31) XP → ZP X′　(32) X′ → X WP　(33) [XP ZP [X′ X WP]]
p.116

Two differences from traditional X-bar: (1) it has lexical items dominated by the minimal level rather than being it; (2) it enforces projection through all levels regardless of specifier/complement — yielding the bloated tree (34). In the book's system, phrasal status is derived, so bar-levels appear only with a specifier; a head with neither complement nor specifier (proper names, pronouns) is automatically both maximal and minimal. 4.3 C-command. A new relation beyond sisterhood, implicated in reflexives.

p.117

The most basic relation is sisterhood (built by Merge); in addition there is c-command (constituent-command):

(35) A node A c-commands a node B iff A's sister either: a. is B, or b. contains B.
(36) [T [Z X Y] [S W R]]

X c-commands only Y; Z c-commands S, W, R; S c-commands Z, X, Y (no reference to precedence); T c-commands nothing (no sister). Reflexives must share φ-features with an antecedent; one required relation turns out to be c-command.

p.118

4.3.2 Reflexive binding.

(37) I shaved myself.
(38) *Myself shaved me.

The old Reflexive Generalization (39: coreferential with another expression; 40: same φ-features) cannot distinguish them. Revised:

(41) A reflexive must be coreferential with a c-commanding expression.

An object is c-commanded by the subject, but not vice versa — so (38) is out (42).

p.119

Could it be precedence instead (43)? No:

(44) The man I saw left.
(45) *The man I saw shaved myself.

I precedes myself but the sentence is bad. The man I saw is a constituent ((46)–(49) tests), so I is inside it and does not c-command myself (50/51).

p.120

I does not c-command myself (a node above it excludes the reflexive), though it precedes it. We therefore reject the precedence version and keep the c-command version.

(52) *My mother hated myself.

My is inside subject my mother, so it doesn't c-command the reflexive. 4.3.3 NPIs. Words like any, ever need a negative element:

(53) *I wanted any cake.　(54) I didn't want any cake.
p.121
(55) *I saw him ever.　(56) I didn't see him ever.
(57) NPIs must occur in a negative sentence.
(58) No one wanted any cake.　(59) *Any boy saw no one.

Both are negative sentences with any, but (59) is ungrammatical. Revised:

(60) NPIs must be c-commanded by a negative element.
(61) *The picture of no one hung upon any wall.

(59): subject any boy isn't c-commanded by no one. (61): no one is inside the constituent (62) so doesn't c-command any. The relevant notion is structural (c-command), not linear or semantic.

p.122

4.3.4 Summary. C-command explains reflexives and NPIs (and pronouns in the exercises). It is a diagnostic tool for structure, added to the constituency tests. 4.4 Ditransitives. The transitive structure (complement = first Merge, specifier = second) is in trouble with three-argument predicates.

p.123

4.4.1 Three-argument predicates.

(63) Benjamin gave the cloak to Nathan.
(64) Nathan received the cloak from Benjamin.

Three participants ⇒ three-place predicates. 4.4.2 Ternary or binary branching? Two analyses of the ditransitive VP.

p.124
(65) [V′ gave [NP the cloak] [PP to Nathan]] (ternary — unavailable under binary Merge)
(66) binary V-recursion: gave [V,uN,uN,uP]

For (66), give has [uN, uN, uP]; involves recursion of the V level (worrying, since previously only adjuncts recursed, and Goals — unlike adjuncts — get a θ-role). Order of checking is urgent: if P checks first, wrong order predicted:

(67) ??Benjamin gave to Lee the cloak.
p.125
(68) a. *Benjamin gave to Lee it.　b. Benjamin gave it to Lee.

The two analyses differ on constituency: V-recursion treats verb+object as a constituent, ternary doesn't. VP-Preposing test:

(69) …and [run away] he did.
(71) …and [give the cloak to Lee] he did.
(72) *…and [give the cloak] he did to Lee.

(72) is bad ⇒ no evidence verb+object is a constituent.

p.126
(73) a. Who gave the cloak to Lee? b. *Benjamin (did) to Lee.
(74)–(77) co-ordination of NPs / verbal projections / APs
(78)/(79) *non-constituents co-ordinated

Co-ordination must be used carefully: (80) [Lee's youngest] and [Dawn's oldest] son — special intonation suggests ellipsis (81). For ditransitives (82) [gave the cloak] and [sent the book] to Lee is marginal with a pause ⇒ deletion (83). No constituency evidence for verb+object, favouring ternary — yet another binary pattern (object+PP a unit) emerges next.

p.127

4.4.3 A binary alternative. Co-ordination provides evidence:

(84) Sam gave [the cloak to Lee] and [the magic chalice to Matthew].

No phonological pause (unlike before), so the cloak to Lee looks like a plain constituent. Structure (86): PP is complement of a head "?" whose specifier is the object; "?P" is the verb's complement.

p.128
(87) *…and [the cloak to Lee] he gave.

No movement evidence for "?P", but independent factors (VP-preposing sensitive to category) may rule (87) out. Three hypotheses: ternary, Goal right-adjoined to V (binary), and the "?P" (binary). Only the third is consistent with the co-ordination evidence. To distinguish further: double object constructions (ditransitives lacking a preposition).

p.129
(88) a. Benjamin gave Lee the cloak. b. Calum sent Nathan the binoculars. c. Lee showed Benjamin the unicorn.
(89) corresponding prepositional sentences
(90) Benjamin gave [Lee the cloak] and [Nathan the chalice].
(91) *Emily showed himself Benjamin in the mirror.
(92) Emily showed Benjamin himself in the mirror.

An asymmetry in reflexive binding. Ternary (93) and V-adjoined (94) wrongly predict (91) good, because Benjamin c-commands himself.

p.130

The "?P" structure (95) makes the right prediction: Benjamin does not c-command himself. PP-goal cases can't decide (the preposition always blocks c-command), so (96) is consistent with either analysis ((97)/(98)). Only Double Object constructions discriminate, supporting the extra "?" constituent.

p.131

For simple ditransitives we adopt the "?P" too (99). 4.4.4 Causatives and little v. What is "?"? The approach: a syntactic relation mimicking causality. English has causative-like structures:

(100) a. Emily caused Benjamin to see himself in the mirror. … (101) a. Emily showed Benjamin himself in the mirror.

The cause paraphrases resemble the simple ditransitives.

p.132
(102) delete to → move see next to caused → replace caused-see with showed.
(103) Georges mange.　(104) Pascale fait manger Georges.

French: verb precedes subject in causatives — a Movement operation; trace marked with angled brackets.

p.133
(105) Pascale fait manger Georges <manger>.
(106) Chichewa -chit-its- (do-cause) … (107) verb -gw- moves into the higher complex (make-fall).

"?" is really a projection of the verb that moves into a higher causal head, notated little v. Trees (108)/(109) show movement of show to v.

p.134

show means "see" but must move into a verb meaning "cause". The causal verb is a light verb; the analysis is a VP-shell. The VP has no overt verb, only a trace (a "ghost", not pronounced). Extends to PP goals (110).

p.135

Selectional features: show = [V, uN, uP], little v = [v, uN] (111). V checks [uP] via PP; the unchecked [uN] projects to V′ and is checked by the specifier; little v merges with the whole VP and projects [uN] to v, checked by the Agent NP. We ensure little v projects not via selection (v assigns no θ-role to VP) but via a Hierarchy of Projections.

p.136
(112) v > V　(113) *[vP v NP] (ruled out)

Whenever there is a little v, it has a VP complement; vP is an extension of the VP projection. The analysis keeps a single binary Merge and links ditransitives to causatives, at the cost of a new operation Move. The book adopts Merge + Move and explores its consequences (more movement evidence in Chs.5–7).

p.137

4.5 Linking. How to constrain the order of checking? With show (two features), (114) is correct; the reversed (115) would generate:

(116) *Nathan showed to Benjamin it.

Since PP-closer-to-verb requires movement unavailable to pronouns, the only parse has arguments in base position ⇒ the object's base position is Spec of the verb, not complement.

p.138

VP-shell puts Agent in Spec-vP, Theme in Spec-VP; transitives put Agent in Spec-VP, Theme in complement-of-V. Compatible, but the Theme has no uniform syntactic representation — the Linking Problem (children would have to learn structures per predicate). If each θ-role had a fixed configuration, children could predict structure from θ-roles. Hence the UTAH:

(117) UTAH: Identical thematic relationships between predicates and their arguments are represented syntactically by identical structural relationships when items are Merged.
p.139

UTAH rules out classes of analyses; a guiding hypothesis (like Binary Branching). Assuming all Agents are in Spec-vP, transitive burn becomes (118), paraphrasable as (119) "X causes Y to burn". The Theme is the daughter of VP in both cases; the Goal = daughter of V:

(120) a. NP daughter of vP → Agent; b. NP daughter of VP → Theme; c. PP daughter of V → Goal.
p.140

UTAH also fixes the order of checking: the reversed structure (121=115) gives no interpretation for the PP (not a daughter of V). It predicts distinct structures for unergatives and unaccusatives:

(122) Unergatives (run, laugh, jump): single Agent, daughter of vP.
(123) Unaccusatives (fall, collapse, wilt): single Theme, daughter of VP.
p.141

Unaccusatives are assumed to have a vP whose v is semantically non-causal (no Agent specifier), to maintain the Hierarchy of Projections. Italian evidence:

(124) Molte ragazze telefonano. (unergative)
(125) Molte ragazze arrivano. (unaccusative)

telefonare = Agent; arrivare = single Theme.

p.142

The famous diagnostic is auxiliary selection:

(126) Molte ragazze hanno telefonato. ('have')
(127) Molte ragazze sono arrivate. ('be', participle agrees with subject)

Different auxiliaries and different participle agreement — exactly what UTAH predicts (returned to in Ch.6).

p.143

4.6 The architecture of the system. A derivation successively applies Merge, Adjoin, Move to syntactic objects. Inputs are lexical items (phonological, semantic, syntactic features); a collection is a numeration. Select takes items onto a workspace.

(128) Step 1 Select A; Step 2 Select B.
(129) Step 3 Merge A&B; Steps 4–6 Select C, D, Merge.
(130) Step 7 Adjoin outputs of Steps 3 & 6.
p.144

A derivation that terminates with all uninterpretable features checked converges; otherwise it crashes. Concrete numeration {saw, v, Sam, Benjamin}: Step 1 Merge saw+Benjamin; Step 2 Merge v (Hierarchy of Projections), v's [uN] projects; Step 3 Move saw to v (obligatory); Step 4 Merge Sam, checking v's [uN].

p.145

Merge is simple; Move copies part of the tree and merges the copy elsewhere, leaving a trace. One output interfaces with meaning — the Conceptual-Intentional (CI) system — via Logical Form (LF), an Interface Level (usually the terminal object). The object feeding the Articulatory-Perceptual (AP) system is reached through the point of Spellout, yielding Phonetic Form (PF) (distinct from the Spellout point).

p.146

Architecture: numeration → Select → workspace → recursive Merge/Move/Adjoin → one object to CI (LF), another to AP (Spellout). The system is highly derivational; rival theories use interacting constraints instead. 4.7 Summary.

p.147
(131) [XP [XP [Spec [X′ X Complement]]] Adjunct]
(132) A c-commands B iff A's sister is B or contains B.
(133) v > V (Hierarchy of Projections fragment)

Ditransitives are a layered/shell VP with a little v taking VP as complement; v–VP is governed by the Hierarchy of Projections, not selection.

p.148
(134) UTAH (as above).
(135) a. NP daughter of vP → Agent; b. NP daughter of VP → Theme; c. PP daughter of V → Goal.

Children associate verbal semantics with situations, then use UTAH to determine syntactic structures. All VPs share a skeleton: a little v + VP complement; two v's — one with a specifier (causal/agentive), one vacuous (unaccusatives), kept to maintain the Hierarchy of Projections.

p.149

The system is derivational: a sentence is grammatical iff a well-formed derivation exists whose final object has no unchecked uninterpretable features (though it may still be unacceptable for semantic or parsing reasons). A summary table maps phenomena (word-order variation, reflexives, NPIs, ditransitive co-ordination, ditransitive word order, unaccusative/unergative differences) to explanations (linearization, c-command, VP-shell, V-to-v movement + Hierarchy of Projections, UTAH).
