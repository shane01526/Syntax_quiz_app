本章引入子句的第三層、也是最高層結構。第 5、6 章已把子句切成兩半：下半部（本質上是 VP）負責述語的題旨性質（thematic properties），屬於詞彙投射；上半部（TP）負責語氣、時制、體（mood, tense, aspect）等非題旨語意，屬於功能投射。本章再加一層比 T 更高的功能投射 CP，它與 T 層性質相近，但所負責的語意不是「事件」本身，而是整個命題的語意地位——這個命題該被當成「陳述」還是「提問」。

為什麼要再加一層？——本章的核心問題意識

到第 6 章為止，我們建立的最高層是 TP。但有一個現象 TP 解釋不了：當一個句子被「裝進」另一個句子裡（內嵌 embedding）時，往往會冒出一個 that。例如「I claimed she was pregnant」整句是一個 TP，但內嵌的部分前面可以加 that。這個 that 不屬於任何 VP 或 TP 的成員，也不分派題旨角色，那它在結構上是什麼？本章主張它是一個新的功能中心語 C（標句詞），投射出 CP，位於 TP 之上。整章就是在論證 CP 層的存在、它的內部特徵（子句類型 [Q]/[Decl]），以及它如何解釋一連串看似無關的現象：主句問句的倒裝、PRO、ECM、提升、德語 V2 等。

8.1.1 標句詞 that

支持「子句頂端還有一層」的證據其實很容易找到。在先前討論子句內嵌時，許多例句都有一個帶 that 的替代式。這個 that 是選擇性出現在內嵌子句的開頭：

(1) I claimed she was pregnant. 我聲稱她懷孕了。（無 that）
(2) I claimed that she was pregnant. 我聲稱她懷孕了。（有 that） (1)(2) 意義完全相同，差別只在 that 的有無。這帶出一個結構問題：that 究竟是跟前面的動詞 claimed 黏在一起（屬於主句的東西），還是跟後面的子句 she was pregnant 黏在一起（屬於內嵌子句的東西）？這個問題不能靠直覺回答，必須用客觀的成分測試來判定。Adger 接下來用三種測試（假分裂、被動移位、右向外置）一致地證明：that 屬於後面的子句。
書頁 290

測試一：假分裂（pseudoclefting）。假分裂句的結構是「What … was ＿＿」，能放進那個空格的字串必為一個成分。

(3) ✓ What she thought was that the poison was neutralized. 她所想的是「毒已被中和」。 「that the poison was neutralized」整串可以放進假分裂的焦點位（was 之後）。既然能被假分裂凸顯，它就是一個單一成分——也就是說 that 和後接的 TP「the poison was neutralized」綁在一起，構成我們即將命名的 CP。
(4) * What she thought that was the poison was neutralized. 這句嘗試把 that 留在前面（緊跟 thought）、只把 TP 推到焦點位。結果不合法。這正說明 that 無法和它的 TP 拆開——若 that 真的屬於前面的動詞，這句應該要合法才對。(3) 合法、(4) 不合法的對比，第一次證明 that 與 TP 同屬一個成分。

測試二：被動移位（passivization）。被動句會把受詞子句搬到句首。觀察 that 是否「跟著一起搬」。

(5) Everyone claimed that the poison was neutralized. 每個人都聲稱毒已被中和。（主動原句）
(6) ?That the poison was neutralized was claimed by everyone. 被動化把整個子句移到句首時，that 一起被帶到句首。如果 that 屬於主句動詞，它就不會跟著子句跑。標 ? 是因為這種主語位的子句稍嫌沉重、語感略勉強，但結構上合法——關鍵是 that 與 TP 不可分離地一起移動。
(7) * The poison was neutralized was claimed that by everyone. 這句反過來嘗試：只把 TP 移到句首，把 that「擱留（strand）」在原處。完全不合法。移位測試的邏輯是「只有成分能整體移動」，而 (7) 顯示你不能把 that 從它的 TP 上剝下來——再次坐實 that 與 TP 是一個不可分割的成分。

測試三：右向外置（rightward movement / extraposition）。that+TP 也能整體越過介系詞片語向右移。

(8) ✓ It was claimed by everyone that the poison was neutralized. that+TP 整體被外置到 PP「by everyone」之後，且 that 仍在子句最前端 → 三度證明它是一個可整體移動的成分。
(9) * It was claimed that by everyone the poison was neutralized. 這句把 that 單獨留在 by everyone 之前、TP 移到更後面 → 不合法。又一次顯示 that 不能脫離它的 TP。三種測試結論一致：that 與後接子句構成一個成分。

像 claim, think, believe, say, decide 等許多動詞都可接由 that 引介的子句補語。這種引介子句的詞，就叫做標句詞（complementizer）。對某些動詞而言，標句詞是強制的：

(10) * Jason whispered the phoenix had escaped.
(11) ✓ Jason whispered that the phoenix had escaped. whisper 這類動詞（不同於 claim）不允許省略標句詞：(10) 沒有 that 就壞了，(11) 有 that 才合法。這顯示 that 的「選擇性 vs 強制性」是由選擇它的動詞決定的詞庫性質，不是一條一律的句法規則。這個觀察為後面「零標句詞 ∅」的論證鋪路。

在其他語言裡，所有子句補語都強制要有標句詞。蓋爾語（Gaelic）：

(12) ✓ Thuirt mi gu bheil i tinn. (Gaelic)　“I said that she was ill.” say-[過去] 我 that 是 她 病 → 我說她病了。
(13) * Thuirt mi bheil i tinn. (Gaelic) 蓋爾語的標句詞 gu 是強制的：缺了就不合法。這說明「標句詞」不是英語特有的怪現象，而是跨語言普遍存在的句法成分；只是各語言對它「可不可省」的參數設定不同。
書頁 291

法語亦然：標句詞 que 強制出現。

(14) ✓ J'ai dit qu' elle était malade. (French)　“I said that she was ill.”
(15) * J'ai dit elle était malade. (French) 法語缺 que 不合法 → 與蓋爾語一樣是「標句詞強制」型語言。三種語言（英、蓋、法）合起來建立了「標句詞」這個跨語言範疇的經驗基礎。

關鍵概念：that 是哪一個範疇？——它不是指示詞 D

確定了 that 與 TP 構成成分後，下一個問題是：that 屬於哪個句法範疇？一個直覺的猜測是 D（限定詞），因為英語的 that 也能當指示詞（that book）。但 Adger 用三個論證推翻這個猜測，主張它是一個全新的範疇 C。

(16) * I said this he left.
(17) * Jason knew those Medea had cast the spell. 論證①：如果標句詞 that 真的是指示詞 D，那麼其他指示詞（this、those）應該也能出現在同一位置。但 (16)(17) 顯示它們完全不行。這代表標句詞位置上的 that 並非指示詞——它只是「碰巧長得跟指示詞 that 一樣」的同音詞。
  • 論證②（跨語言）：其他語言的標句詞與其指示詞系統毫無關係。法語標句詞是 que，但其指示詞是 ce/cela/ça；蓋爾語標句詞是 gu，指示詞卻是 seo/sin。若標句詞普遍是 D，這種「標句詞與指示詞長得完全不同」就難以解釋。
  • 論證③（語言內部）：英語還有別的標句詞（如 whether、for，下面會看到），它們與限定詞系統毫無相似之處——更說明標句詞是獨立範疇。

基於這三點，多數語言學家把 that 及其他標句詞分析為一個新範疇 C（Complementizer）。C 與 TP 合併（Merge）並投射，產生 (18) 的結構：

(18) CP / \ C TP / \ Subject T̄ / \ T vP
這張樹要記牢：CP 是 C 的最大投射，C 取 TP 為補語（complement）。換言之，C 把整個 TP「包起來」成為更大的單位 CP。這就是子句的最高層結構，也是本章其餘所有分析的骨架——後面討論的問句倒裝、PRO、提升等，都發生在這個 C–T 的架構之內。

8.1.2 陳述句與疑問句

書頁 292

C 必為功能範疇（functional category）而非詞彙範疇（lexical category），因為它不分派 θ-角色。標句詞的語意作用比較抽象，但直覺上是：它指示聽者該如何看待其子句所表達的命題——是當成一個單純的「事實陳述」，還是當成一個「對事實的提問」。於是除了陳述用的 that，也有引介問題的標句詞：

(19) ✓ Jason asked whether the potion was ready. Jason 問藥水是否好了。
(20) ✓ Medea wondered if the potion was ready. Medea 想知道藥水是否好了。 whether 和 if 引介的子句是「間接問句」。它們和 that 一樣站在子句最前端、引介整個子句，因此 Adger 接下來要證明它們也是標句詞 C，只是攜帶的子句類型特徵不同（問句 vs 陳述）。

證據一：互補分布。whether/if 與 that 呈互補分布（complementary distribution）——意思是同一個位置上只能有一個，不能並存：

(21) * Jason asked whether that the potion was ready.
(22) * Medea wondered if that the potion was ready. whether/if 與 that 不能同時出現。在生成語法裡，「兩個成分互補分布」是它們競爭同一個句法位置的典型證據。既然 that 佔的是 C 位，whether/if 不能與它共存，就表示三者都搶同一個 C 位 → whether/if 也是 C。

證據二：成分性（假分裂）。whether/if 與其後子句構成一個成分，正如 that 一樣：

(23) ✓ What Jason asked was whether the potion was ready.
(24) * What Jason asked whether was the potion was ready.
(25) ?What Medea wondered was if the potion was ready.
(26) * What Medea wondered if was the potion was ready. (23)(25) 能整體假分裂（whether/if + 子句一起進焦點位），(24)(26) 不能把 whether/if 留在原處 → 與 that 的 (3)/(4) 行為完全平行。結論：whether 和 if 都是標句詞 C。

由於語意作用不同，我們用一個可詮釋句法特徵來區分它們。問句子句稱疑問（interrogative），非問句稱陳述（declarative），子句的這個身分就是它的子句類型（clause type）。

特徵設定：假設特徵 [clause-type]，其值為 [Q]（疑問，Q 是 question 的記號）或 [Decl]（陳述）。此特徵在 C 上是可詮釋的（interpretable）——意思是它對語意有貢獻，正是它讓陳述句被解讀成陳述、問句被解讀成提問。that 帶 [Decl]，whether/if 帶 [Q]。
書頁 293

接 C 補語的述語，可在詞庫條目中標記其 c-選擇（c-selection）需求：

(27) ask, [V, uC]
(28) exclaim, [V, uC] 兩者都帶不可詮釋特徵 [uC]，意思是「我需要一個 CP 補語」。這個 u 特徵必須被一個真的 CP 檢核掉，否則推導崩潰——這就是「動詞要求子句補語」這件事在特徵理論裡的形式化。

但問題來了：述語似乎還能決定它的補語是疑問還是陳述：

(29) * Ron asked that the potion was ready.
(30) * Medea exclaimed if the potion was ready. ask 只能接疑問 [Q] 子句（接 that 就壞），exclaim 只能接陳述 [Decl] 子句（接 if 就壞）。問題是：這個「挑子句類型」的能力，該寫進句法的 c-選擇，還是只是語意的 s-選擇（s-selection）不相容造成的？（註：(30) 對某些母語者在「每當藥好，Medea 就驚呼」的解讀下可接受，但那是 8.3 的附加語結構，與此無關。）

Adger 論證答案是語意（s-選擇）。關鍵證據來自 maintain：它像 exclaim 一樣拒絕 [Q] 子句，但它的行為更能揭露問題的本質。

(31) ✓ Julie maintained that the barman was sober.
(32) * Julie maintained if the barman was sober. maintain 接陳述 that 子句可以、接疑問 if 子句不行——表面上像是 maintain「c-選擇 [Decl]」。
(33) ✓ Julie maintained her own ideas over the course of the argument. maintain 也可以接一個 DP 補語（her own ideas）。
(34) * Julie maintained her own questions over the course of the argument. 這是決定性的一步。her own ideas 和 her own questions 在句法上完全相同（都是 DP，結構一模一樣），差別只在語意——questions 的語意是「問題」。既然 (34) 不合法只能歸因於「maintain 不能與語意上指涉問題的東西結合」這個語意不相容，那麼同樣的語意解釋也能排除 (30)（exclaim 接疑問子句）。既然語意解釋在 (34) 是不可避免的，把它也用在 (30) 就最為精簡——故「挑子句類型」是 s-選擇而非 c-選擇。
書頁 294

有了「述語一律 c-選擇 CP」這個結論，可以進一步推論：連「可省略 that」的情形，其實底層也是一個 CP，只是 C 位被音韻空的標句詞（zero complementizer, ∅）佔據。這樣較高的述語就永遠選一個 CP，不必區分「有時選 CP、有時選 TP」：

(35) VP / \ said CP / \ that/∅ TP he was happy
that 與 ∅（零標句詞）都佔 C 位 → said 永遠 c-選擇一個 CP。這個「零標句詞」的概念非常重要，它讓理論保持一致（述語一律選 CP），也預告了後面控制子句（空 C[null]）與 ECM 的分析手法。

關鍵問題：主句也是 CP 嗎？

既然內嵌子句是 CP，那主句（matrix sentence）呢？它也是 CP，還是只是 TP？此問題沒有定論：有人為求精簡（economy）主張簡單句只是 TP（沒有更多結構的證據就別假設更多結構）；有人為求子句的一致性（uniformity，內嵌子句既然是 CP，主句也該是）主張主句也是 CP。Adger 的策略是：先證明至少某些主句確定是 CP，再推廣到全部。

確定是 CP 的主句：主句問句（yes/no questions）：

(36) Had the potion boiled over?
(37) Did the magic work? 這類問句（因最自然的回答是 yes/no 而稱 yes/no questions）與其陳述對應句的差別很明顯：助動詞跑到主語之前。傳統稱主語–助動詞倒裝（Subject-Auxiliary Inversion, SAI）。問題是：助動詞「往前跑」跑到哪裡去了？如果主句有 CP 層，答案就現成了——跑到 C 位。
書頁 295

SAI 的分析 ＝ T-to-C 移位。若主句是 CP，分析就很漂亮：投射 T 的特徵束（即時制）整個移入 C 位，原理就跟「英語最高助動詞移入 T」「法語動詞群移入 T」一樣（第 5 章）。我們假設 Tense 附加（adjoin）到 C。樹 (38) 顯示 Had the potion boiled over? 的推導：完成式助動詞 have 先移到 T 形成附加結構，T 再升入 C，全靠連續的中心語移位（head movement）達成。

但為什麼 T 裡的複合體要移到 C？答案再次是特徵強度（feature strength）——這正是本書解釋一切移位的統一手法：

  1. 假設 T 帶一個不可詮釋、未賦值的子句類型特徵 [uclause-type: ]（方括號裡空白表示「還沒拿到值」）。此時結構是：
    (39) [TP Subject Perf+T[uclause-type: ] … ]
  2. 當 C[Q] 與這個 TP 合併，C 上可詮釋的 [Q] 把 T 的子句類型特徵賦值為強 Q（記為 Q*，星號＝強）。
    (40) C[Q] [TP Subject Perf+T[uclause-type:Q*] … ]
書頁 296
  1. 由於 T 上的 Q 是強的，依本書通則，強特徵會強制 T 升入、與它所 Agree 的特徵（C 上的 [Q]）形成局部關係，產生 (41) 的最終結構（T+C 合在 C 位），並帶著 T 內部的一切（含 have）一起上移。這就是倒裝的來源——助動詞之所以跑到主語前，是因為它隨 T 一起被吸進了 C。

這個分析還順帶正確預測了 do-support（助動詞 do 插入），這是它的一大優點：疑問 C 觸發 T-to-C 移位後，T 就不再 c-統制下方的小 v，兩者間無法形成「鏈（chain）」，使得「時制發音規則（Rule for Pronouncing Tense）」無法套用；於是 do 作為最後手段（last resort）插入，承載孤立的時制特徵：

(42) Did the potion boil over? (43) 樹：T[past, uclause-type:Q*] 升到 C 與 C[Q] 合併；TP 內主語 the potion 留在 spec-TP。T 升出後與下方 v 失去 c-統制關係 → 無法把 [past] 發音在主動詞上 → do 插入承載 [past]，發成 did。注意：do-support 不是另外規定的，而是「T 移走→鏈斷→最後手段」這一連串獨立機制的自動結果，這正是分析優美之處。

SAI 的存在強烈顯示至少主句 yes/no 問句有 CP 層。Adger 進一步假設所有主句都有 CP；陳述主句的 C 位被一個音韻空的標句詞佔據，它把 T 的 [uclause-type] 賦值為 Decl。Q 與 Decl 的關鍵差別是：Decl 是弱的，弱特徵不觸發移位，所以陳述句沒有倒裝。

(45) CP / \ C[Decl] TP / \ the potion T̄ / \ T[past,uDecl] vP (boil over)
陳述主句的結構：C 帶弱的 [Decl]，T 留在原位，沒有倒裝。把這張 (45) 和問句的 (43) 對照看，整個「陳述 vs 疑問」的差異就被化約成單一參數——C 賦給 T 的子句類型值是強（Q*，→倒裝）還是弱（Decl，→不動）。

特徵縮寫慣例 (46)：(a) 可詮釋的 [clause-type:Q]（C 上）→ 縮寫 [Q]；(b) 未檢核未賦值的 [uclause-type: ]（剛生成的 T 上）→ 縮寫 [uclause-type]；(c) 已檢核、賦強值的 → 縮寫 [Q*]。這套縮寫與第 6 章為「格位」設計的縮寫平行。英語通常忽略 [Decl] 不寫，因其句法效應微不足道（弱、不動）。

8.1.3 主語子句

到目前為止，標句詞引介的子句都出現在補語位（動詞後）。但它們也出現在主語位——這就帶來新的分析難題：

(47) [That Jason abandoned Medea] stinks! 「Jason 拋棄了 Medea」這件事真糟糕！
(48) [That the golden thread would show Jason his path through the labyrinth] was obvious.
(49) [That I am here] proves [that I care]. (49)：兩個論元都是子句——主語是子句、賓語也是子句（prove 是兩元述語）。
(50) [That Zeus was so promiscuous] astounded the other gods.
(51) [That Medea murdered Jason] didn't surprise anyone. 這組例句展示了子句作主語的各種搭配：(47)(48) 是一元述語（stink, be obvious，只有一個論元，就是這個子句主語）；(49) 是兩個論元都是子句；(50)(51) 是子句主語配上非子句賓語。共同點：句首那個 that 子句佔據了通常由主語 DP 佔的位置。問題是——它真的就站在 spec-TP（一般主語位）嗎？
書頁 298

有兩種競爭的分析：

兩種分析的對立

  • 分析一（(52)，直覺版）：子句主語就位於 spec-TP（標準主語位），且和一般主語一樣，由 VP 內部位置提升而來。簡單、符合直覺。
  • 分析二（(53)，Adger 採用）：子句位於 spec-CP（更高的位置），而真正的 spec-TP 主語位被一個空成分（empty element / null pronoun）佔據。這個空成分才是「真主語」，句首的 CP 只是在語意上與它連結。

分析二乍看複雜、多假設了一個看不見的空成分，為何要採用它？因為下面一連串證據顯示：這些「子句主語」的行為根本不像普通主語。

書頁 299

Adger 把這些待考察的子句中性地稱為初始 CP（initial CPs）（避免預設它們在哪個位置）。採分析二的理由如下：

證據一：初始 CP 不做 SAI。普通 DP 主語能參與主語–助動詞倒裝，初始 CP 卻不行：

(54) * Did that Medea killed her children upset Jason?
(55) * Has that we have arrived back at our starting point proved that the world is round? 如果初始 CP 真的就站在 spec-TP（和普通主語同位），那麼把它做成問句（SAI）應該沒問題。但 (54)(55) 全壞。這暗示初始 CP 並不在普通主語位——它在別的地方，而那個地方與 SAI 不相容。

證據二：初始 CP 內可有 DP 主語，但不能再嵌一個初始 CP。

(56) ✓ [That [the answer] is obvious] upset Hermes.
(57) * [That [that the world is round] is obvious] upset Hermes.
(58) * [That [whether the world is round] is unknown] bothered Athena. (56)：初始 CP 內部的主語可以是普通 DP（the answer），完全合法。(57)(58)：但初始 CP 內部的主語不能又是一個初始 CP（that…/whether…）。如果初始 CP 和 DP 真的同分布，這種不對稱就無法解釋。「普通 DP 主語」與「初始 CP」分布不同 → 它們在不同的句法位置。

證據三（跨語言）：荷蘭語的特殊代名詞。在荷蘭語裡可以直接看到，初始 CP 不在主語位——因為主語位被一個特殊代名詞 dat 佔走了：

(59) Dat hij komt, dat is duidelijk. (Dutch)　“That he will come is clear.” that 他 來，dat（那） 是 清楚 → 他會來，這是清楚的。
(60) That he is coming e is clear. 荷蘭語在初始 CP 之後出現一個指示代名詞 dat（「那」），它才是真正佔主語位的東西。Adger 主張英語結構與荷蘭語完全相同，只是英語對應 dat 的成分是空的（null，記為 e）。(60) 就是英語的底層結構：初始 CP 在前、一個看不見的空代名詞 e 在主語位。荷蘭語把這個位置「念出來」，英語則不念。
書頁 300

分析二的具體運作（推導）：空代名詞先在 spec-vP 合併（取得它需要的位置），升到 spec-TP 檢核格位與 EPP；接著 Merge C，再把先前已經建好的那個 CP Merge 進 spec-CP。這最後一步的合併由 C 的 [uC] 選擇特徵觸發（C 需要一個 CP 在它的指定語）：

(61) CP / \ CP C̄[uC] "that he / \ is coming" C TP / \ e T̄ is clear

分析二的三大紅利（為什麼值得多假設一個空成分）：

  • 紅利①：保住 EPP 的定義。可繼續主張「T 的 EPP 是一個 [uD] 特徵」（必須由一個 D/DP 來滿足）。因為滿足 spec-TP 的是空代名詞（一個 D），而不是 CP。若採分析一（讓 CP 直接進 spec-TP），就得重新定義 EPP 成「可被 DP 或 CP 滿足」，弄髒了理論。
  • 紅利②：解釋為何初始 CP 不做 SAI（(54)(55)）。規定初始 CP 結構所需的這個特殊 C[uC] 與 [Q] 特徵不相容，所以有初始 CP 就不能有 Q、也就不能 SAI。
  • 紅利③：解釋為何初始 CP 不能嵌初始 CP（(57)(58)）。見下。

對照組——初始 CP 內部若是普通 DP 主語，該 DP 出現在標句詞之後（spec-TP），與初始 CP（在 spec-CP）位置明顯不同：

(62) [That Jason arrived] infuriated Medea.
(63) [Whether Agamemnon had triumphed] was unknown. 普通 DP 主語（Jason、Agamemnon）緊跟在標句詞 that/whether 之後 → 它在 spec-TP。初始 CP 則在更外層的 spec-CP。位置不同，分布自然不同。
書頁 301

初始 CP 既然是與一個特殊空 C[uC] 的投射合併，我們就永遠不會在 that 或 whether 之後找到初始 CP（紅利③）。但這也產生一個新謎題——下面這些也該被排除：

(64) * [[That Jason had arrived] C e was obvious] C e infuriated Medea.
(65) * I said that that Jason was jealous annoyed Medea. 解法：規定這個空 C[uC] 只能出現在主句位置。(64)(65) 都在內嵌子句裡用到這個空 C → 違反限制，被正確排除。Adger 坦言這只是把描述形式化，尚非真正的解釋，但至少抓對了模式。

對照：Ross (1967) 的另一條途徑——Internal S Condition

歷史上最早處理初始主語的途徑之一（Ross 1967 博論）採分析一（初始 CP 真的在 spec-TP），把它的怪異句法歸因於一條剖析原則：禁止一個 CP 被夾在另一個 CP 的音韻材料之間（兩側都有上層 CP 的語音材料，就叫「內部 internal」）。這條原則稱 Internal S Condition。

看它怎麼運作 (66)：若初始 CP 在 spec-TP，而 T-to-C 移位發生（does 跑到 C），那麼初始 CP 兩側都有主句 CP 的語音材料（左邊 does、右邊 annoy Medea），它就「內部化」了 → 被禁止，於是預測 (54)(55) 不合法。

(66) CP / \ C(does) TP / \ [CP that Jason T̄ is jealous] (T) ...annoy Medea
書頁 302

但 Internal S Condition 有致命反例：

(68) ✓ Believing [that the world is flat] gives one some solace. 這裡主句 CP 的材料 believing 和 gives one some solace 分別在內部 CP「that the world is flat」的左右兩側，照 Internal S Condition 應該被排除，但這句完全合語法。一個會錯殺合法句子的原則就不可靠 → Adger 放棄 Ross 途徑，改採 spec-CP 分析（分析二）。
(69) * e is obvious. 不過分析二也留下自己的遺留問題：如果主語位真有一個空代名詞 e，為何不能讓 e 單獨當主語、得到 *e is obvious？這個問題要等到 8.2.8（用 [null] 格位）才能解答。

8.2 非限定 CP 補語

本節是全章篇幅最大、邏輯最環環相扣的一節，探討各類述語的非限定（non-finite）子句補語——也就是動詞接的「to do…」這類沒有時制的子句。Adger 的策略是：先回顧第 4 章 θ-理論的兩個鐵則，再用它們像手術刀一樣，把表面相似的四種結構（控制、ECM、for 子句、提升）一刀刀切開。

書頁 302–303

回顧的邏輯起點：由於所有不可詮釋特徵都必須被檢核，而 c-選擇特徵與 θ-角色相連，可推得述語的所有 θ-角色都會被分派出去。再加上 UTAH（題旨角色與句法位置的對應），可得：一個 DP 唯有在 UTAH 指定的論元位置（argument position）合併，才能被解讀為論元。這也解釋了虛主詞：

(70) ✓ There arrived a new actor. 有個新演員到了。 虛主詞 there 不領 θ-角色，純粹是為了滿足 EPP 而插入。所有 θ-角色都在 unaccusative 動詞 arrive 的 vP 內分派完畢（a new actor 在那裡領到角色）。虛主詞插在「無 θ-角色的位置」（如 spec-TP）。
(71) * The doctor arrived a new actor. 不能把一個真實 DP（the doctor）放在虛主詞該在的位置，因為那個位置沒有 θ-角色可領，而真實 DP 必須領 θ-角色（見下 73b）。對照 (70)：there 可以待在無 θ 位是因為它不需要 θ-角色。
(72) * Medea murdered. murder 是兩元述語（施事＋受事）。若一個 DP 能領兩個 θ-角色，Medea 就能先在 murder 的姊妹位領 Theme、再升到 spec-vP 領 Agent，把兩個角色都包了，這句就該合法。但它不合法 → 證明一個 DP 只能領一個 θ-角色。
(73) 兩條 θ-理論鐵則（整節分析的基石，務必記牢）：
a. 一個 DP 只能領一個 θ-角色（θ-準則）。→ 用來論證「為何需要 PRO」「為何 Medea 不是提升來的」。
b. 一個 DP 必須領一個 θ-角色。→ 用來論證「為何 seem 句不能用 PRO」。
Adger 特別提醒：整節分析的強度，取決於這兩條通則的正確性；若它們將來被推翻，分析就得重做（延伸閱讀有反 θ-理論的途徑）。

8.2.1 PRO 與控制子句

書頁 304

第 5 章曾用非限定的 to 來論證 TP 的存在。現在看這類非限定子句補語：

(74) Medea tried [to poison her children]. Medea 試圖毒死她的孩子。
(75) Odysseus planned [to hear the sirens]. Odysseus 計畫聽海妖的歌聲。 注意內嵌動詞（poison, hear）都是兩元述語，需要一個施事。而那個施事在語意上被主句主語綁定：(74) 想下毒的人「就是 Medea，不會是別人」；(75) 要聽海妖的「就是 Odysseus」。問題是——這個施事在句法上是「誰」？它沒有出現任何字啊。

而且，下層子句的主語不能用任何外顯的詞填上去：

(76) * Medea tried [Medea to poison her children].
(77) * Medea tried [the nurse to poison her children].
(78) * Odysseus attempted [Odysseus to hear the sirens].
(79) * Odysseus attempted [the helmsman to hear the sirens]. 無論填回主句主語本身（Medea/Odysseus）還是填別人（the nurse/the helmsman），全都不合法。所以下層主語位「必須是空的，但又必須存在」（因為 poison 要派施事角色出去）。

推論出 PRO。poison 派 Agent 與 Theme。Theme 給了 her children。Agent 給誰？不能是主句的 Medea——她已經從 tried 領了一個 θ-角色，依鐵則 (73a) 不能再領第二個。因此下層 spec-v 必須有一個看不見的成分來領這個 Agent；整個子句再由主句動詞 tried 領一個 θ-角色。這個空主語就命名為 PRO：

(80) Medea tried [to PRO poison her children]. PRO 在下層 spec-v 合併、領 Agent，並被主句主語 Medea 控制（controlled）（即解讀上與 Medea 同指）。含 PRO 的子句因此稱控制子句（control clause）。注意：PRO 是被「θ-準則」逼出來的——不是憑空假設，而是「Agent 一定要派出去、Medea 又不能領第二角色」的邏輯必然結果。
書頁 305

PRO 也可以不被任何 DP 控制，此時得到任指（arbitrary）解讀，類似正式英語的 one（「人們、任何人」）：

(81) PRO to steal hexes from witches is dangerous. （任何人）去偷女巫的咒語是危險的。
(82) If one were to steal hexes from witches, then that would be dangerous.
(83) It is not allowed to PRO perjure oneself. (81)(83) 的 PRO 沒有先行語可控制，故解讀為「泛指任何人」。本章不深究 PRO 何時受控制、何時任指（那是語意/控制理論的問題），重點放在 PRO 的句法分布——它能出現在哪、不能出現在哪。這個分布問題會用「格位」來解答（8.2.4）。

8.2.2 ECM 子句

控制子句不允許外顯 DP 主語（見 76–79）。但有另一類非限定補語恰恰相反——它們允許、甚至要求外顯主語：

(84) We believed Zeus to be omnipotent. 我們相信 Zeus 是全能的。
(85) No one expected Agamemnon to win.
(86) We believed that Zeus was omnipotent.
(87) No one expected that Agamemnon would win. believe/expect 是「個體–命題」兩元述語。限定版 (86)(87)（接 that 子句）證明了這一點：相信的是「Zeus 全能」這個命題，而 Zeus 是該命題內部的主語。假設 believe 的 θ-派發性質不變，則 (84)(85) 中 Zeus/Agamemnon 也應是下層子句的主語，而非主句的賓語。
(88) We believed [Zeus to be omnipotent].
(89) No one expected [Agamemnon to win].
(90) We believed [him to be omnipotent]. 關鍵觀察：把下層主語換成代名詞，得到的是賓格（accusative：him，而非主格 he）。這很奇怪——它明明是「下層子句的主語」，照理該是主格，怎麼會是賓格？這個「主語卻帶賓格」的矛盾，正是這個構造名稱的由來。
書頁 306
(91) No one expected [him to win]. 傳統名稱 ECM（Exceptional Case Marking，例外格位標記）——「例外」就在於：一個明明是下層主語的 DP，卻例外地拿到賓格。舊名 Raising-to-Object（賓語提升）。Adger 保留 ECM 這名稱，但他後面發展的分析其實會顯示，這個賓格的來源和一般「動詞–賓語」的賓格是同一個機制（v[acc]），並不真的那麼「例外」。
(92) * We believed to PRO be omnipotent.
(93) ✓ No one expected to PRO win. 事情更複雜：有些允許外顯主語的動詞禁止 PRO（believe，見 92），有些卻允許 PRO（expect，見 93）。這逼我們區分三類動詞。
三類非限定補語動詞（本節要靠格位＋EPP 區分它們）：
① 強制 PRO、禁外顯主語：try, attempt, endeavour。
② 允許外顯主語、禁 PRO：believe, consider, think, assume。
③ PRO 與外顯主語皆可：want, expect, prefer。
這三類在語意上各成一群，但 Adger 要問的是：它們的句法規格差在哪？答案將是「選 CP 還是 TP」「C 帶什麼格位」的差異。

8.2.3 非限定子句中的 C（標句詞 for）

既然限定子句有標句詞（that），合理推測非限定子句也該有，以求平行。證據就是看似介系詞的 for：

(94) I intended [for Jenny to be present]. 我本意是要 Jenny 在場。
(95) [For you to do that] would be a mistake. 非限定子句裡出現了外顯主語（Jenny / you），且其前有個看起來像介系詞 for 的成分。它到底是介系詞，還是非限定子句的標句詞？
書頁 306–307

先排除「PP 分析」。初看可把 for Jenny 當成一個 PP (96)。若如此，這個 PP 應能被分裂或話題化 (97)(98)。這些句子確實合法——但語意不對：在 (97)(98) 裡 Jenny 被解讀成受惠者（benefactive）（「為了 Jenny 的好處」），而不是「在場的人」。

(96) I intended [for Jenny] [to be present].　（PP 分析）
(97) It was for Jenny that I intended to be present. （受惠者解讀：為了 Jenny，我打算在場——在場的是「我」）
(98) For Jenny, I intended to be present. (97)(98) 合法但意思變了：for Jenny 變成受惠者 PP、在場的人是說話者「我」。這說明原句 (94) 其實是結構歧義的：一個讀法 for Jenny 是受惠者 PP；另一個讀法（我們要的）Jenny 是非限定子句的主語、在場的人是 Jenny。可見「PP 分析」只對應到受惠者那個讀法，抓不到主語那個讀法。

假分裂證明 for Jenny 屬於內嵌子句（而非主句的獨立成分）：

(99) ✓ What I arranged was for Jenny to be present. 「for Jenny to be present」可整體進假分裂焦點位 → for + 主語 + to 子句是一個成分（CP）。
(100) * What I arranged for Jenny was to be present. （在「安排 Jenny 在場」的讀法下）不能把 for Jenny 切出來、只留 to be present。這顯示 for 緊接子句主語、與整個 CP 構成成分——與限定子句的 that 行為完全平行。結論：for 是非限定子句的標句詞 C。
書頁 308
(101) CP / \ for[C] TP / \ Jenny T̄ (to be present)

關鍵觀察：有 for 的非限定子句，主語必須是賓格，PRO 被排除：

(102) ✓ Jason intended for him to learn magic.（賓格 him ✓）
(103) * Jason intended for he to learn magic.（主格 he ✗）
(104) * Jason intended for PRO to learn magic.（PRO ✗）
(105) * For to do that would be a mistake.（主語不可省 ✗） for 子句的主語必須外顯、且必須賓格（him 而非 he），PRO 不行。這個「for ↔ 賓格」的緊密關聯是下一步分析的核心：for 這個 C 帶有賦賓格的能力。

C 如何決定下層主語的格位？延伸第 6 章已有的格位檢核機制即可：一個較高的功能中心語可與它 c-統制的最近 DP 建立格位檢核關係。假設 for 所選的非限定 T 根本沒有格位特徵，那麼賦格的工作就落到 for 身上：

  1. (106) v[acc] V DP[3,sing,fem,case]　——小 v 帶 [acc]，賓語 DP 帶未賦值的 [case]。
  2. (107) v 把賓格賦給賓語 DP（標準的賓語賦格）。
  3. (108) 接著 Merge 主語（也是代名詞）、非限定 T、標句詞 for[acc]。
書頁 309
  1. (109) for 就像 v 賦賓語格位那樣，把賓格賦給子句主語。
  2. (110) 拼讀結果：for … to him … see her（一個非限定子句裡兩個賓格：主語 him + 賓語 her）。
(111) I arranged [for him to see her]. 一個非限定子句裡有兩個賓格（him、her），分別由 for 和 v 賦予。這正是 (110) 推導的結果。

但 (110) 的詞序不對：照推導，非限定 T（to）會排在賓格主語之前（*to him），與正確的 for him to 不符。解法沿用第 6 章的關鍵區分：T 的格位特徵與 EPP 特徵是分離的，且驅動移位的是 EPP，不是格位。非限定 T 與限定 T 唯一的差別只是「沒有格位特徵」；它的 EPP 仍在，會把 him 吸到 spec-T，再 Merge for，就得到正確詞序：

(112) [CP for [DP[acc] T[uD*]] … (him) v[acc] V DP[acc]] → for him to (him) see her 本小節的精髓：非限定子句主語的位置由 EPP 決定（him 升到 spec-T，故排在 to 前），格位由 C（for）決定（賓格）。位置與格位由兩個獨立特徵分管——這與第 6 章修訂後的限定子句分析完全平行。

8.2.4 空格位與 PRO

書頁 309–310

現在回頭解決控制子句的問題：是什麼機制保證 try 類動詞的控制子句裡一定是 PRO？Adger 的核心直覺非常漂亮：正如「格位」調控 for 子句裡賓格 DP 的出現，「格位」也調控控制子句裡 PRO 的出現。PRO 不是特殊的神祕物，它只是「帶某種特殊格位的普通代名詞」。

為此，在英語格位系統（[nom]、[acc]、[gen]）之外新增第四種格位 [null]（空格位）。它的形態效應是：讓帶它的代名詞不發音——正如 [nom] 的形態效應是讓 [3,sing,masc] 發成 he，[null] 的形態效應就是讓代名詞發成「空」。

於是主張：try 類動詞選一個帶 [null] 的空標句詞 C[null]。控制子句的推導：

  1. (113) [vP pronoun v[acc] V Object[acc]]　——先建好 vP，spec-vP 裡有個普通代名詞。
  2. (114) Merge 非限定 T（有 EPP、無格位）；EPP 把代名詞升到 spec-TP。
  3. (115) Merge 空 C[null]，把代名詞的格位賦值為 [null]。
  4. (116) Merge 動詞 try；拼讀時，帶 [null] 的代名詞發成空 → 結果就是我們寫成 PRO 的東西：try PRO to (PRO) see him。
書頁 310

注意：所謂「PRO」其實是一個經 EPP 驅動移位、最終因 [null] 格位而不發音的代名詞特徵束。這個分析立刻解釋了一連串事實：

(117) * Medea tried her to leave. 規定 [null] 只能是代名詞的特徵（不能是全 DP 的）。所以 try 的空 C[null] 只能跟代名詞搭配。
(118) * Medea tried ∅[null] Jason[case] to leave. 若放一個全 DP（Jason）當下層主語，它的 [case] 無法被只賦 [null] 給代名詞的空 C 檢核 → 格位沒檢核掉 → 崩潰。這解釋了「控制子句不能有外顯 DP 主語」（呼應 76–79）。
(119) * I arranged for PRO to see her. 反過來，for 子句為何不能有 PRO？因為 for 會把任何代名詞主語賦成 [acc] 而非 [null]，帶 [acc] 的代名詞一定要發音（him），不可能發成空的 PRO → (119) 無法生成。「for ↔ 賓格外顯」與「null C ↔ PRO」是同一套格位邏輯的兩面。
書頁 311

小結：非限定子句主語分布之謎的答案是——非限定 T 不檢核格位；下層主語的格位改由「選它的 C」（控制的 C[null] 或 for[acc]）檢核，主語的位置則由非限定 T 的 EPP 決定。與限定子句的修訂分析一致。

還剩一類沒交代：那些沒有外顯標句詞、卻只能接外顯 NP 主語（不能 PRO）的 believe/assume 類：

(123) * I assumed to be innocent.

Adger 採用的替代分析：ECM 動詞選的是 TP，不是 CP

(129) vP / \ Subj v̄ / \ v[acc] VP / \ V TP ← believe 選的是 TP（無 CP） / \ DP[acc] T̄ ← 這個 DP 是下層主語，卻被上層 v[acc] 賦賓格 / \ T vP

1. 控制子句＝由空 C[null] 引介的 CP；[null] 把代名詞主語賦為 [null] → 強制 PRO、禁外顯 DP。
2. ECM 子句＝裸 TP，由較高動詞選；上層 v[acc] 跨子句賦下層主語賓格 → 禁 PRO（拿不到 [null]）。

插曲：解決一個技術問題（書頁 319–321）

提升分析有個漏洞要補。我們說 seem「能選 TP」。若它選的是限定 TP（而非非限定 TP），推導竟也會錯誤地收斂（converge），預測出不合法的 (158)：

(158) * Agamemnon seems [TP (Agamemnon) has left]. 問題的根源：依目前的 Agree 規則，即使 Agamemnon 的格位已在下層限定 T 檢核過，它的格位特徵仍能與上層 T 的格位 Agree（Agree 只要求特徵 match）。於是它又能滿足上層 EPP、提升上來 → 錯誤地讓「限定 TP 提升」變合法。我們需要一個機制阻止這件事。

  1. (159) seem [TP … T[uclause-type: ] …]　——這個下層限定 T 的子句類型特徵沒有 C 來賦值。
  2. (160) 繼續推導：上層 T[uclause-type: ] … seem [TP … T[uclause-type: ] …]。兩個特徵 match，但子句類型不是純有無特徵（privative）——它需要一個值，而現在沒有值可給，所以還無法檢核。
  3. (161) Merge 上層 C[Decl]：C 把上層 T 的 [uclause-type] 賦值為 Decl。
書頁 320–321

關鍵的兩道封鎖：(a) 由特徵配對的局域性（Locality of Feature Matching），上層 C[Decl] 只能管到上層 T，不能跨層去賦值下層 T；(b) 由 (162) 延伸條件（Extension Condition）——「句法操作只能作用於樹的根投射（root projection）」——已被賦值的上層 T 也不能回頭去賦值更深處的下層 T（因為上層 T 此刻已不在樹根）。結果：下層 T 的 [uclause-type] 永遠檢核不掉，推導正確地崩潰。

這個插曲的重大結論：限定 T 才有的 [uclause-type] 特徵，與格位機制互動，封鎖了「主語從限定 TP 提升」。推而廣之，這也封鎖了 seem（與 ECM 的 believe）接限定 TP 的可能。因此得到一條漂亮的普遍限制：子句補語只能是 CP，或非限定 TP——不能是限定 TP。

8.2.7 賓語控制（Object Control）

轉向三元述語帶子句論元的情形：

(177) Jason persuaded Medea to desert her family. Jason 說服 Medea 拋棄她的家庭。
(178) Agamemnon forced Zeus to leave the school.
(179) Jason persuaded [Medea] [to desert her family].　（三論元結構，正確）
(180) Jason persuaded [Medea to desert her family].　（兩論元＝ECM 結構，錯誤） 從 θ-結構看，persuade/force 明顯是三元述語（Jason、Medea、to desert her family 三個論元）。所以結構必是 (179)（Medea 與後面子句分開、各為一論元），而非 (180)（Medea 與子句合成一個 ECM 式補語）。下面用兩個獨立的句法論證坐實這點。
書頁 324

論證一：虛主詞分布。EPP 只在 T 上，故虛主詞只能出現在 spec-TP（主語位），不能當賓語：

(181)/(182) * I found there. / * I destroyed there.（there 不能當賓語）
(184) ✓ I expected there to be a problem.（ECM：there 是下層主語，合法）
(185) * I persuaded there to be a problem.（persuade：there 落在賓語位，不合法）
(186) ✓ I believed there to be three books on the subject.
(187) * I ordered there to be three books on the subject. 對比的邏輯：ECM（expect/believe）後面那個 DP 是下層子句主語（spec-TP），所以可以是 there；persuade/order 後面那個 DP 是主句賓語（不是主語位），所以不能是 there。若兩者結構相同，就不該有這個對比。對比存在 → persuade 的 DP 是賓語、ECM 的 DP 是主語。
書頁 324–325

論證二：被動化的釋義關係。把下層子句被動化，看意思變不變：

(188) Jason expected the doctor to treat Medea.
(189) Jason expected Medea to be treated by the doctor. ECM：(188)≈(189) 互為釋義。因為 expect 的補語是「一整個子句」，被動只發生在子句內部，expect 的補語（命題）內容沒變——主動子句與被動子句本來就大致同義。

8.3 附加語子句

書頁 327–328

子句除了當補語、當主語，還能當附加語（adjunct），用來修飾主句所表達的命題。這裡有一個重要的機制對比：補語靠 Merge 整合進結構（由特徵檢核需求觸發、強制的）；附加語則靠 Adjoin（附加）整合（不由特徵檢核觸發，因此是選擇性的——句子沒有它照樣完整）。附加語 CP 分兩大語意類別：時間關係、因果／理由關係。

第一類：時間附加語（before / when / after）：

(199) Before the executioner arrived, Hephaestus had escaped. 劊子手到達之前，Hephaestus 已經逃走了。
(200) When the executioner arrived, Hephaestus was asleep.
(201) After the executioner left, Hephaestus wept.
(202)–(204) Hephaestus had escaped, before the executioner arrived.（可移到主句之後） 時間附加語可以放在主句的任一側（句首或句尾），這種位置自由度正是「附加語」（而非補語）的典型特徵——補語的位置是固定的。
(205) Medea thought that, after the executioner had left, Hephaestus would be relieved. 關鍵的定位證據：時間附加語可以出現在「內嵌標句詞 that」與「子句主語 Hephaestus」之間。這個夾縫位置正好是 TP 的邊緣（在 C 之下、在 spec-TP 之上）→ 證明時間附加語附加（adjoin）於 TP。
(206/207) 附加於 TP（外部結構）： 附加語子句內部結構： TP CP / \ / \ [CP after…] TP after TP Hephaestus… the executioner had left

第二類：因果／理由附加語（because / since / so that）：句法行為與時間附加語幾乎相同。

(208) Medea was happy, because she had got the highest marks.

In Chapters 5 and 6 we saw that the clause splits roughly into two parts: the lower part has to do with the thematic properties of the predicate (essentially the VP), and is associated with the lexical projection, while the upper part seems to have to do with non-thematic semantic properties such as mood, tense, and aspect. This upper part is associated with functional projections.

In this chapter we will introduce one further clausal layer, which is higher still than T and its associated projections. This layer is also functional in nature, and bears close affinities to the T layer. However, the semantic properties associated with this higher level do not have to do with tense/aspect and other properties of the event picked out by the verb. Rather they have to do with the semantic status of the whole proposition.

8.1 Complementizers — 8.1.1 that

The evidence for the syntactic presence of this higher layer is actually fairly easy to come by. You may have noticed, whenever we have discussed the embedding of one clause inside another, that many of the examples given had an alternative. So, in addition to (1), we have (2):

(1) I claimed she was pregnant.
(2) I claimed that she was pregnant.

The word that in these examples optionally occurs at the start of the embedded clause. We can use simple constituency tests to show that this word forms a constituent with the following clause, rather than with the embedding verb.

p.290

For example, pseudoclefting shows that that forms a constituent with the following TP, rather than with the preceding verb:

(3) What she thought was that the poison was neutralized.
(4) *What she thought that was the poison was neutralized.

Similarly, movement tests show that it is possible to raise that plus its following TP, once again suggesting they are in constituency. Interestingly, it is not possible to strand that, leaving it behind by moving the TP on its own. The simple active sentence in (5), for example, can be passivized as in (6). When this happens, note that the that is carried along with the rest of the clause to the front of the sentence:

(5) Everyone claimed that the poison was neutralized.
(6) ?That the poison was neutralized was claimed by everyone.
(7) *The poison was neutralized was claimed that by everyone.

It is also possible to move that plus its TP to the right, across a prepositional phrase:

(8) It was claimed by everyone that the poison was neutralized.
(9) *It was claimed that by everyone the poison was neutralized.

Verbs like claim, think, believe, say, decide, and many more take a clausal complement that may be introduced by the word that in English. Words which introduce clauses in this way are called complementizers. With some verbs, there is no option: the complementizer is obligatory:

(10) *Jason whispered the phoenix had escaped.
(11) Jason whispered that the phoenix had escaped.

In other languages, complementizers are obligatory for all clausal complements:

(12) Thuirt mi gu bheil i tinn. (Gaelic) “I said that she was ill.”
(13) *Thuirt mi bheil i tinn. (Gaelic)
p.291
(14) J'ai dit qu' elle était malade. (French) “I said that she was ill.”
(15) *J'ai dit elle était malade. (French)

The appearance of the word that in English in constituency with the embedded clause motivates the idea that it is a syntactic element which merges with TP, but does not tell us anything about the category of this element. An obvious possibility is that the relevant category is D, since, in English, the word that also serves as a demonstrative. However, this approach fails for a number of reasons. First, no other demonstrative can occur in this position with the appropriate reading:

(16) *I said this he left.
(17) *Jason knew those Medea had cast the spell.

This strongly suggests that the word that is not a demonstrative in these contexts; secondly, other languages have complementizer elements that are nothing like any element of their demonstrative system. As can be seen from the examples above, in French, the complementizer is que. However, the demonstratives are ce/cela/ça; similarly, in Gaelic the demonstratives are seo/sin, but the complementizer is gu. The third argument against treating that as a D is that there are other complementizers in English, which look nothing like any part of the determiner system. We will look at these in more detail below.

For these reasons, most linguists analyse the word that, and other complementizers, as elements of a new syntactic category, C (for Complementizer). C merges with TP and projects, giving structures like (18): [CP C [TP Subject [T̄ T vP]]].

8.1.2 Declaratives and interrogatives

It is clear that C must be a functional rather than a lexical category, as it does not assign a θ-role. The semantic function of complementizers is a complex matter, but intuitively, a complementizer indicates how the hearer should think of the proposition expressed by its clause: the two main possibilities are whether the clause should be thought of as a simple statement of fact, or as a question about the facts. So we find complementizers introducing questions, as well as the cases with simple propositions that we have already met:

(19) Jason asked whether the potion was ready.
(20) Medea wondered if the potion was ready.

We can see that whether and if are in complementary distribution to that in English:

(21) *Jason asked whether that the potion was ready.
(22) *Medea wondered if that the potion was ready.

and that they are in constituency with their following clause:

(23) What Jason asked was whether the potion was ready.
(24) *What Jason asked whether was the potion was ready.
(25) ?What Medea wondered was if the potion was ready.
(26) *What Medea wondered if was the potion was ready.

This suggests that if/whether are also complementizers. Given their different semantic function, we can distinguish that from whether/if by means of an interpretable syntactic feature. The common term used for clauses which are questions is interrogative while clauses which are non-questions are said to be declarative. The status of a clause as interrogative or declarative is its clause type. We will assume a feature [clause-type] whose values are either [Q] (interrogative) or [Decl] (declarative). These features are interpretable on C.

p.293

We can state the c-selectional requirements of predicates which take a clausal complement in the lexical entries for the predicates as follows:

(27) ask, [V, uC]
(28) exclaim, [V, uC]

However, we still need to distinguish between these two classes of predicates, since the predicate seems to be able to determine whether its complement is interrogative or declarative:

(29) *Ron asked that the potion was ready.
(30) *Medea exclaimed if the potion was ready.

The question is whether this information should be built into the c-selectional properties of the selecting predicate, or whether it is a simple s-selectional fact. There is some evidence that the semantic (s-selectional) approach is the correct one. There are verbs like maintain which behave just like exclaim in rejecting a [Q] complement clause:

(31) Julie maintained that the barman was sober.
(32) *Julie maintained if the barman was sober.
(33) Julie maintained her own ideas over the course of the argument.
(34) *Julie maintained her own questions over the course of the argument.

Since, from a syntactic point of view her own ideas and her own questions are identical, we need to appeal to a semantic incompatibility to rule out (34). Since we need to appeal to this semantic explanation in other cases, it makes sense to appeal to it to rule out examples like (30) as well.

p.294

Given these lexical entries, we are led to assume that the cases with an optional that are actually cases with a zero complementizer, so that the higher predicate always selects a CP (35) [VP said [CP that/∅ [TP he was happy]]].

This position naturally leads to another question: do simple matrix sentences contain complementizers? There is no agreed-upon answer. Some linguists have argued that, in the interests of economy, a simple sentence is just a TP; others have argued, on the basis of uniformity across clauses, that simple sentences must be CPs. It is certainly the case that some simple sentences are CPs. We can see this with matrix questions:

(36) Had the potion boiled over?
(37) Did the magic work?

These simple interrogatives (often termed yes/no questions) contrast with their declarative counterparts: the auxiliary appears, not after the subject, but before it. Traditionally, this construction is known as Subject-Auxiliary Inversion (SAI). If we assume that matrix sentences are CPs, then we have a simple analysis: the collection of features that projects T (i.e., tense) moves into the C position.

p.295

We will assume that tense adjoins to C (38). What we have here is a situation where the perfect auxiliary is moved to T, creating an adjunction structure with T. Tense has then raised into C, again creating an adjunction structure. The resulting structure is created by successive steps of head movement. To motivate movement, we appeal to strength of features. If we assume that T bears an uninterpretable unvalued clause-type feature [uclause-type: ], then this feature can check with a [Q] feature on matrix C. On the assumption that the clause-type feature on T when valued as Q is strong in English, T will move to C, carrying along everything inside it.

(39) [TP Subject Perf+T[uclause-type: ] … ]
(40) C[Q] [TP Subject Perf+T[uclause-type:Q*] … ]
p.296

Given that Q on T is strong, it raises into a local relation with the feature that it Agrees with, giving (41) as the final structure. Interestingly, this analysis also predicts the correct occurrence of do-support: Interrogative C triggers movement of T to C. Once this movement has taken place, T no longer c-commands little v. This means that a chain cannot be formed between them, and so the Rule for Pronouncing Tense cannot apply; do-support applies as a last resort instead.

(42) Did the potion boil over?

The existence of SAI structures strongly suggests that a CP layer is present in at least matrix yes/no questions. We will assume further that CP layers are present in all matrix sentences, but that, for matrix declaratives, the topmost C position is occupied by a phonologically null complementizer, which values the uninterpretable clause-type feature on T as Decl. The difference between Q and Decl is that the latter is weak, and so no movement takes place.

p.297
(44) The potion boiled over.

In future structures we'll abbreviate clause-type features as: (46a) [clause-type:Q] → [Q]; (46b) [uclause-type: ] → [uclause-type]; (46c) [uclause-type:Q*] → [Q*]. We will usually ignore [Decl] since its syntactic effects are trivial in English.

8.1.3 Subject clauses. The examples we have seen so far of clauses introduced by complementizers have all been in complement position. We also find clauses in subject positions:

(47) [That Jason abandoned Medea] stinks!
(48) [That the golden thread would show Jason his path through the labyrinth] was obvious.
(49) [That I am here] proves [that I care].
(50) [That Zeus was so promiscuous] astounded the other gods.
(51) [That Medea murdered Jason] didn't surprise anyone.
p.298

The first two examples here are cases of one-place predicates: stink and be obvious. Example (49) is a case where a two-place predicate allows both of its arguments to be clausal, while (50) and (51) show cases where we have a clausal subject but a non-clausal object. A natural analysis (52) assumes that the clausal subject is in the specifier of TP, raised from a VP internal position. An alternative analysis (53) treats the first clause as being in the specifier of CP, with an empty element in the subject position. The empty element is the true subject, and the CP is associated with it semantically.

p.299

In order not to prejudge the analysis, let us call the CPs we are interested in here initial CPs. The reason that the second analysis has been proposed is that these CPs do not behave like other subjects. For example, they do not undergo Subject Auxiliary Inversion:

(54) *Did that Medea killed her children upset Jason?
(55) *Has that we have arrived back at our starting point proved that the world is round?

Similarly, while it is possible to have a DP subject in an initial CP, it is not possible to have an initial CP in an initial CP:

(56) [That [the answer] is obvious] upset Hermes.
(57) *[That [that the world is round] is obvious] upset Hermes.
(58) *[That [whether the world is round] is unknown] bothered Athena.

Finally, in other languages, it is clear that an initial CP is not in subject position, because a special pronoun form appears (Dutch):

(59) Dat hij komt, dat is duidelijk. “That he will come is clear.”
(60) That he is coming e is clear.
p.300

The null pronoun Merges with the bar-level projection of little v and then raises to the specifier of TP, checking case and the EPP feature of T. We then Merge C, and then a previously constructed CP. This Merger needs to be triggered by a selectional feature of C, so the matrix C itself has to have a uC feature (61). One theoretical advantage is that it allows us to maintain our idea that the EPP feature on T is an uninterpretable [D] feature of T. We also have a way of encoding the fact that SAI does not occur with initial CPs. The proposal also predicts why initial CPs can't occur inside other initial CPs:

(62) [That Jason arrived] infuriated Medea.
(63) [Whether Agamemnon had triumphed] was unknown.
p.301
(64) *[[That Jason had arrived] C e was obvious] C e infuriated Medea.
(65) *I said that that Jason was jealous annoyed Medea.

If we say that the null C[uC] is restricted to matrix-clause positions, then (64) and (65) will be ruled out. An alternative approach (Ross 1967) argued that the odd syntax of initial CPs was due to a general parsing principle which prohibited CPs from being internal to other CPs — the Internal S Condition (66).

p.302

However, there are sentences which seem to fall foul of the Internal S Condition, but which are perfectly well formed:

(68) Believing [that the world is flat] gives one some solace.
(69) *e is obvious.

For this reason, we will adopt the specifier of CP analysis for initial CPs. The most worrying problem is how to rule out (69); we will return to this in section 8.2.8.

8.2 Non-finite CP complementation. Recall that lexical items are specified with c-selectional features, associated with θ-roles. Since all uninterpretable features have to be checked, all the θ-roles of a particular predicate will be assigned.

p.303

This theory made sense of expletive elements (70) There arrived a new actor; (71) *The doctor arrived a new actor. A DP can never be interpreted as an argument unless it has Merged in an argument position. A DP is assigned only a single θ-role (72) *Medea murdered. Let us sum up:

(73) a. A DP may receive only one θ-role. b. A DP must receive a θ-role.
p.304

8.2.1 PRO and control clauses.

(74) Medea tried [to poison her children].
(75) Odysseus planned [to hear the sirens].
(76) *Medea tried [Medea to poison her children].
(77) *Medea tried [the nurse to poison her children].
(78) *Odysseus attempted [Odysseus to hear the sirens].
(79) *Odysseus attempted [the helmsman to hear the sirens].

There must be a null element Merged in the specifier of little v. For convenience, we term this null subject PRO:

(80) Medea tried [to PRO poison her children].
p.305

In sentences like (80) PRO is said to be controlled by the matrix subject. Clauses with PRO are Control Clauses. PRO may also occur where it is not controlled, in which case it is arbitrary in interpretation:

(81) PRO to steal hexes from witches is dangerous.
(82) If one were to steal hexes from witches, then that would be dangerous.
(83) It is not allowed to PRO perjure oneself.

8.2.2 ECM clauses. There are non-finite complement clauses which allow overt subjects:

(84) We believed Zeus to be omnipotent.
(85) No one expected Agamemnon to win.
(86) We believed that Zeus was omnipotent.
(87) No one expected that Agamemnon would win.
(88) We believed [Zeus to be omnipotent].
(89) No one expected [Agamemnon to win].
(90) We believed [him to be omnipotent].
p.306
(91) No one expected [him to win].
(92) *We believed to PRO be omnipotent.
(93) No one expected to PRO win.

The traditional name is ECM (Exceptional Case Marking). We must distinguish three types of verb: obligatory PRO (try, attempt, endeavour); overt subject but no PRO (believe, consider, think, assume); both PRO and overt subjects (want, expect, prefer).

8.2.3 C in non-finite clauses. Given that finite clauses can be headed by complementizers, non-finite clauses may be as well:

(94) I intended [for Jenny to be present].
(95) [For you to do that] would be a mistake.
p.307

At first blush, a plausible approach treats for Jenny as a PP (96). This predicts clefting/topicalization (97)(98), which are well formed but have a benefactive reading. The sentence is structurally ambiguous. Pseudoclefting shows the for Jenny string is part of the embedded clause:

(99) What I arranged was for Jenny to be present.
(100) *What I arranged for Jenny was to be present.

Distributionally, for in non-finite clauses occurs immediately before the subject and forms a constituent with CP, exactly parallel to that. We classify for as a complementizer.

p.308

(101) [CP for[C] [TP Jenny [to be present]]]. Whenever the complementizer for appears, the clause must have an accusative subject; PRO is ruled out:

(102) Jason intended for him to learn magic.
(103) *Jason intended for he to learn magic.
(104) *Jason intended for PRO to learn magic.
(105) *For to do that would be a mistake.

We need a way of letting C determine the case of the subject. Assuming the non-finite T that for selects has no case feature, a case-checking relation can be established between for and the subject (106)–(108).

p.309

Just as v valued the case of the object, for will value the case of the subject (109)(110). This means we get a non-finite clause with two accusatives:

(111) I arranged [for him to see her].

The word order is derived because the EPP feature of T causes him to raise to its specifier before for is Merged (112). Non-finite T lacks a case feature, so the case is determined by something else; word order is still determined by the EPP property of T.

8.2.4 Null case and PRO. Just as case regulates the appearance of an accusative DP in for clauses, it is case that regulates the appearance of PRO in control clauses. We extend our case features to include [null], whose morphological effect is to ensure the pronoun is unpronounced.

p.310

A verb like try selects a CP headed by a complementizer with this [null] feature, itself unpronounced (113)–(116) → try PRO to (PRO) see him. This accounts for:

(117) *Medea tried her to leave.
(118) *Medea tried ∅[null] Jason[case] to leave.
(119) *I arranged for PRO to see her.
p.311

The case of the subject of an infinitival clause is checked by some other element — the C which selects the non-finite T. The position of the subject is determined by the EPP feature on non-finite T.

8.2.5 Cross-clausal case checking and ECM. How do we deal with cases with no overt complementizer but where the embedded subject must be overt NP?

(120) We believed him to be the headmaster.
(121) I assumed him to be innocent.
(122) *We believed to be the headmaster.
(123) *I assumed to be innocent.

One approach (124) believe, [V, uC[acc]] assumes a complex feature bundle, contrary to our usual assumption.

p.312

The alternative analysis: the case feature of the embedded subject is checked by the usual case properties of the embedding verbal complex. ECM verbs may take a DP object (126) Anson believed the report; (127) Ron expected the sack — accusative checked by v[acc] (128). As an alternative to a null C, ECM verbs select a non-finite TP whose subject enters a case-checking relationship with the little v above believe (129).

p.313

This explains why ECM verbs do not allow PRO (130) *Anson believed PRO to be happy — since little v bears [acc] rather than [null]. A second argument: control clauses and for-clauses pattern with respect to pseudoclefting, contrasting with ECM:

(131) What Medea arranged was for her children to be poisoned.
(132) What Medea attempted was to poison her children.
(133) *What Medea believed was Jason to be a murderer.
(134) What I said was [CP that we would go].
(135) *What I said that was [TP we would go].

(136) 1. Control clauses are headed by a null C[null]. 2. ECM clauses are just TPs, checked by higher v[acc]. 3. for clauses occur with overt accusative subjects, for[acc].

p.314

This might be challenged by non-standard dialects (Belfast, Dundee):

(137) I went to the shop for to get bread.
(138) They are going home for to see their parents.
(139) I tried for to get them.
(140) I wanted Jimmy for to come with me.

The dialectal for seems lower in the structure, attached to T, lacking the accusative case feature. A weakness: we need ECM verbs to select either a finite C or a non-finite TP — a placeholder for a real analysis.

8.2.6 Raising. Single-argument verbs, notably seem and appear:

(141) It seems that Agamemnon is a maniac.
(142) It appears that Hephaestus owns a dragon.
(143) Agamemnon seems to be a maniac.
(144) Hephaestus appears to own a dragon.
(145) Agamemnon attempted to behave well.
(146) *Agamemnon seems [PRO to be a maniac].
(147) *Hephaestus appears [PRO to own a dragon].
p.316

The correct analysis: the subjects in the matrix clauses have got there by movement, from underlying structures where they appear in their θ-positions (148) [it [seems [CP that Agamemnon left]]]. seem does not have a subject θ-role to assign, so we Merge an unaccusative little v (149).

p.317

We could Merge an expletive it (151) It seems that Agamemnon left. We are also interested in (152) Agamemnon seems to have left. Verbs like seem can also Merge with a TP (153). Although to can check its EPP by attracting Agamemnon, it cannot value Agamemnon's case feature, since it lacks a case feature of its own.

p.318

Given that we then Merge in seem and the higher finite T, it becomes possible to check the case feature of the matrix T with that of Agamemnon (154). Agamemnon can move into the specifier of the higher T (155). The classical name for this construction is Subject-to-Subject Raising (or just Raising).

p.319

There are two movements, both driven by the EPP feature of T. Interlude: Solving a technical problem. If we choose a finite TP, the derivation incorrectly converges (156)(157). Although the case feature of Agamemnon has been checked, it still Agrees with the case feature of T.

p.320
(158) *Agamemnon seems [TP (Agamemnon) has left].

One standard approach: case features delete immediately when checked. Another solution relies on the [uclause-type: ] feature: if seem selects a finite TP, this feature must be checked, but can't be checked by its own C (159)–(161). By Locality of Feature Matching and the Extension Condition (162: a syntactic derivation can only be continued by applying operations to the root projection of the tree), the lower T's feature cannot be checked.

p.321

The derivation is correctly predicted to crash. Clausal complements must be either CPs, or non-finite TPs. Back to raising. Raising constructions can be strung together:

(163) Hephaestus appears to have turned out to have left.

(167) *Zeus stinks to be omnipotent.
(168) There seemed to be three men in the garden.
(169) There appears to be a problem with this solution.

8.2.8 Summary and further implications. Non-finite T is specified with an EPP feature but not with a case feature. The case of the subject is determined by the complementizer (null C[null] or for[acc]) or by some element outside the clause (v[acc] for ECM, T[nom] for raising).

(197) [That the world is round] e is obvious.
(198) *e is obvious.

The C head that selects for an initial CP bears a [null] case feature.

(199) Before the executioner arrived, Hephaestus had escaped.
(200) When the executioner arrived, Hephaestus was asleep.
(201) After the executioner left, Hephaestus wept.

(202) Hephaestus had escaped, before the executioner arrived.
(203) Hephaestus was asleep, when the executioner arrived.
(204) Hephaestus wept, after the executioner left.
(205) Medea thought that, after the executioner had left, Hephaestus would be relieved.

These properties suggest that temporal adjunct clauses adjoin to TP (206). They are headed by the temporal complementizer, which takes a TP complement (207).

(208) Medea was happy, because she had got the highest marks.
(209) Because she had got the highest marks, Medea was happy.

(210) Jason became invisible, so that he could escape.
(211) So that he could escape, Jason became invisible.

8.4 Cross-linguistic implications. The core phenomenon is Verb-Second (V2), found in most Germanic languages. The finite verb ends up in the second position, immediately after the first constituent (German):

(212) Ich las schon letztes Jahr diesen Roman.
(213) Diesen Roman las ich schon letztes Jahr.
(214) Schon letztes Jahr las ich diesen Roman. “I read this book already last year.”

The first constituent counts as the topic of the clause.

p.330

Whereas in English the Q value was strong but the Decl value was weak, in German matrix C, when it values [uclause-type] on T, always provides a strong feature (215)(216). Matrix C in German bears a strong topic feature [utop*] (217)(218)(219). This is backed up by subordinate clauses:

(220) Er sagte dass ich schon letztes Jahr diesen Roman las. “He said that I read this book already last year.”

German is, at bottom, an OV language.

(221) Er sagte dass ich schon letztes Jahr [vP diesen Roman gelesen] habe.
